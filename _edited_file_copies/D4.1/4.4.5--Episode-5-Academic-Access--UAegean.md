### Episode 5: Academic Access (UAegean)

Within the **SEDIT-X** use case, **Episode 5: Academic Access** focuses on the use of the **EUDI Wallet** to enable access to selected digital and physical services offered by the **University of the Aegean** to members of the **ERUA alliance**.

At scenario level, this episode contributes to the broader WP4 objective of demonstrating how the EUDI Wallet can support a seamless and trusted traveller journey across multiple domains. While other SEDIT-X episodes address transport and hospitality interactions, Episode 5 extends the same wallet-based approach into the higher-education domain, showing how identity and academic-status-related verification can also support access to university services.

At use-case level, the episode examines how a user may rely on the EUDI Wallet to access selected university services made available by UAegean to ERUA participants. Example services may include:

- access to a workshop or classroom session,
- access to online course material,
- access to selected academic or campus-related digital resources.

The exact scope of the pilot will depend on the specific service or services that the University of the Aegean makes available at the time of piloting. For this reason, the episode is intentionally defined at a high level in this deliverable. The common principle is that access should be granted through a wallet-based interaction, using the EUDI Wallet as the trusted user-facing channel for presenting the required evidence.
