### Episode 1: Smart Airport: Biometric Boarding (UAegean)

Within the SEDIT-X use case, **Episode 1: Smart Airport: Biometric Boarding** represents the airport segment of the traveller journey and demonstrates how the **EUDI Wallet** can be used to support secure, privacy-preserving, and operationally efficient passenger processing across key airport touchpoints.

At scenario level, this episode contributes to the broader WP4 objective of showing that the EUDI Wallet can function as a **common interaction channel** across different service environments, while also illustrating how identity, travel entitlement, and, where applicable, biometric verification can be combined in a way that reduces friction and repeated manual checks. In that sense, the airport episode provides the starting point of the SEDIT-X journey and establishes the operational model that is later extended to urban mobility, ferry transport, hospitality, and academic access.

At use-case level, the episode focuses on the use of the **EUDI Wallet for airport verification and boarding processes**, including passenger identity proofing, presentation of the boarding entitlement, and, in the biometric variant, **1:1 biometric verification without pre-enrolment**. The current SEDIT-X design examines two complementary operational paths. In one path, the traveller uses a wallet-supported **biometric QR credential** issued after prior verification of identity and flight entitlement. In the other path, the traveller presents the necessary wallet-held credentials directly through **proximity verification**, notably the PID and the boarding-pass credential, with the verification result used by airport systems at the relevant control point.

The episode covers the main airport touchpoints where such flows are relevant, including **check-in, baggage drop-off, security screening, and gate boarding**. In the biometric-boarding variant, a live facial capture may be compared in a **1:1 verification model** against the biometric reference associated with the presented credential set, with the objective of enabling a more seamless and contactless passenger journey while remaining aligned with privacy-preserving design choices.
