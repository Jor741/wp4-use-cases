#### Trusted Issuer Lists

### 7.5 Trust Framework and Trusted Issuer Lists

In **UC3: Seamless Digital Traveller Experience (SEDIT-X)**, WP4 relying parties must be able to identify which issuers are recognised as trusted for the purposes of credential verification across **cross-border** and **cross-domain** interactions. This is particularly important because UC3 spans multiple operational contexts, including airport, ferry, hospitality, urban mobility, and university access.

For the **target production model**, issuer recognition is expected to rely on the **eIDAS 2.0 / EUDI Wallet ecosystem trust framework**. For the **APTITUDE pilot**, however, issuer recognition will be operationalised through an **APTITUDE pilot Trust List (TL)** that mimics the structure and role of an eIDAS-style trust list for piloting purposes.

The purpose of the Trusted Issuer List in UC3 is therefore practical: it gives WP4 relying parties, or the **EUDIW Intermediary Service** acting on their behalf, a common reference for determining whether the issuer of a presented credential is one of the issuers recognised within the pilot trust environment.

In this sense, the Trusted Issuer List does **not** by itself replace the full trust-validation process. Rather, it supports one essential part of that process: confirming that the credential issuer is recognised for the pilot and is trusted to issue the relevant credential type.

For UC3, the Trusted Issuer List is expected to support at least the following categories of issuers, where relevant to the pilot scenario:

- **PID issuers / national identity authorities**
- **transport-related issuers**, such as airline or ferry ticketing issuers
- **hospitality-related issuers**, such as booking-reference or stay-related issuers
- **academic or student-attestation issuers**
- **other authorised attestation providers** participating in the pilot

For each trusted issuer, the pilot list should enable the verifier to determine, at minimum:

- the identity of the issuer;
- the trust anchor or pilot trust context under which the issuer is recognised;
- the credential types or roles for which the issuer is trusted; and
- any relevant status information needed to determine whether the issuer remains valid for the purposes of the pilot.

Operationally, when a credential is presented in UC3, the relying party first checks whether the credential issuer is included in the applicable **APTITUDE pilot Trust List** and whether that issuer is trusted for the presented credential type. Only if that condition is satisfied does the verifier proceed with the remaining verification checks defined by the broader trust framework.

This approach is especially important for **cross-border piloting**, since relying parties should not need to establish separate ad hoc trust relationships with each foreign issuer. Instead, the Trusted Issuer List provides a shared pilot reference point that allows issuers from different participating contexts to be recognised in a consistent way.

Overall, in UC3 the Trusted Issuer List should therefore be understood as the **operational issuer-recognition component** of the broader trust framework: it identifies **who is trusted to issue what** within the pilot, while the separate trust-validation process determines whether a particular presented credential should actually be accepted in a given transaction.
