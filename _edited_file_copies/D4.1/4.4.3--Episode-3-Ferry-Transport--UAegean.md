### Episode 3: Ferry Transport (UAegean)

This episode addresses the use of the **EUDI Wallet** in the maritime leg of the **SEDIT-X** journey, focusing on ferry travel from **Piraeus or Rafina** and demonstrating how wallet-based identity and travel credentials can support a more seamless boarding experience.

At a high level, the ferry episode follows the same architectural approach as the other SEDIT-X domains. The traveller uses the wallet as the common presentation channel for maritime travel entitlements and related identity evidence, while an **EUDIW Intermediary Service** acts as the integration layer between wallet protocols and the operator-side booking, ticketing, and boarding environment.

At booking time, the ferry operator or participating travel agency issues a **Ferry Boarding Credential** to the traveller’s wallet. Where applicable, the booking and entitlement flow may also take into account other wallet-held credentials, such as **student status** or **accessibility/disability attestations**, in order to support verified discounts or other special travel conditions.

At the port, the intended operational model is a **proximity-based presentation flow**. In this setting, the traveller presents from the wallet the evidence required for boarding validation. As a baseline, this includes the **PID** as the root identity credential and the **Ferry Boarding Credential** as proof of travel entitlement for the specific route or journey. Where the traveller is embarking with a vehicle, the flow may also include a **mobile Vehicle Registration Credential (mVRC)** or equivalent vehicle-related credential.

The verifier checks that the presented ferry boarding entitlement is valid for the specific journey and, where relevant, that the vehicle-related credential is consistent with the booking context. In this way, the ferry episode extends the broader SEDIT-X design principle of using a small, context-specific set of wallet credentials to support fast, privacy-conscious, and operationally usable verification decisions.
