### Episode 4: Hospitality (UAegean)


The **Hospitality** episode of SEDIT-X explores how the **EUDI Wallet** can support a more seamless, privacy-preserving, and progressively automated hotel journey, from booking to on-site check-in and, where feasible, check-out. Within the broader WP4 scenario, this episode contributes the hospitality domain view of the end-to-end traveller journey and demonstrates how wallet-based identity and booking-related credentials can be used in real operational settings. Detailed per-UC specifications are provided in **Annex A**. 

At a high level, the hospitality episode is organised around two main interaction stages. First, during the **booking stage**, the traveller may use the wallet to provide the identity attributes needed to complete a hotel reservation through a booking portal or travel intermediary. The verified result is then used by the booking environment without fundamentally changing the existing downstream reservation model. Second, after the reservation has been created, a **Booking Reference Credential** is issued to the traveller’s wallet and is later used during **on-site hotel check-in** together with the required identity attributes. 

At the hotel, the intended target flow is a **self-service check-in** interaction. A self-service terminal, such as a tablet or similar device, initiates a verification session through the **EUDIW Intermediary Service**, which generates a presentation request for the traveller’s wallet. The traveller discloses the required identity attributes together with the Booking Reference Credential, and the verified result is used to identify the corresponding reservation and progress the check-in process. Where supported by the participating hotel environment, the hotel or PMS may also update guest details and check-in status automatically. 

A defining characteristic of this episode is that the **exact implementation level may vary by hotel**. The pilot  explicitly recognises that hotel technical environments are heterogeneous and therefore allows different operational variants, ranging from **reception-assisted verification** and **receipt-based assisted flows** to **partially integrated PMS flows** and more advanced **fully integrated self-service flows**. This means that the hospitality episode should be understood as a common wallet-enabled service pattern rather than a single fixed deployment model. 

In scenario terms, Episode 4 shows how SEDIT-X extends the same wallet logic used in transport domains into the hospitality sector: credentials are issued into the wallet, selectively presented when needed, verified through an intermediary-enabled flow, and then used to drive an operational decision. In this case, that decision concerns booking confirmation, reservation retrieval, legal guest-registration support, and hotel check-in progression.
