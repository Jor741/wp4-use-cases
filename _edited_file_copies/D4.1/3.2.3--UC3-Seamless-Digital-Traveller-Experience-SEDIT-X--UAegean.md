### UC 3: Seamless Digital Traveller Experience (SEDIT-X) (UAegean)

## Stakeholder Involvement

The development of the **SEDIT-X** use case has involved a multi-stakeholder process reflecting the cross-domain nature of the pilot. Because SEDIT-X spans airport operations, urban mobility, ferry transport, hospitality, academic access, identity-wallet infrastructure, and payment-related interactions, stakeholder involvement has necessarily included both **service-domain actors** and **technical or coordination actors**. The overall objective of this engagement has been to ensure that the use case design is grounded in real operational conditions, aligned with sector constraints, and feasible within the evolving EUDI Wallet ecosystem. 

At category level, the stakeholders involved or consulted in SEDIT-X include:

- **Transport and mobility stakeholders**, such as airport operators, airline-side operational actors, ferry operators, and urban mobility providers;
- **Hospitality stakeholders**, including hotels, booking platforms, destination management companies (DMCs), and PMS-related actors;
- **Higher-education stakeholders**, especially the University of the Aegean and, more broadly, the ERUA-related academic-access context;
- **Wallet and trust-infrastructure stakeholders**, including EUDI Wallet-related actors, PID and attestation issuers, and the **EUDIW Intermediary Service** role used as the common integration layer in several episodes;
- **Technical implementation and interoperability stakeholders**, including solution providers, integrators, and WP-level technical contributors;
- **End-user and operational stakeholders**, such as travellers, students, and accessibility-related user groups considered in the design of the pilot journey. 

In terms of **engagement methods**, the preparation of SEDIT-X has so far relied primarily on:

1. **Focused workshops and working sessions**, used to explore episode-specific operational models and align the participating parties around target flows and pilot constraints;
2. **Bilateral calls and alignment meetings**, used in particular where episode design depends on the capabilities or constraints of a specific operator or technical environment;
3. **Working papers and iterative written review**, used to consolidate the results of stakeholder discussions into progressively more stable scenario descriptions;
4. **Shared drafting and collaborative document exchange**, used to refine use-case text, episode descriptions, and technical assumptions across WP4 contributors and external operational partners.


Overall, stakeholder involvement in SEDIT-X should be understood as an **iterative co-design process** rather than a one-off consultation exercise. This is particularly important because several episode scopes remain partly dependent on the operational partners available at pilot time, the maturity of integrations across sectors, and the specific services ultimately selected for piloting.
