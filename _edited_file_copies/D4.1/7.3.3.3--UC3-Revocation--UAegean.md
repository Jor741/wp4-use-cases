#### Revocation

### 7.3 Issuance, Verification and Revocation Flows

In **UC3: Seamless Digital Traveller Experience (SEDIT-X)**, revocation is considered part of the broader verification lifecycle, but it is **not a core pilot feature**.

At a general level, WP4 relying parties are expected to verify not only the authenticity and validity of a presented credential, but also, where applicable, whether its status indicates that it should no longer be accepted. In the target EUDI Wallet ecosystem, this is part of the normal trust and status-validation process.

For the purposes of the **APTITUDE pilot**, revocation handling is expected to follow a **Token Status List** , using the relevant status information made available by the issuer or trust framework component. In practical terms, this means that a verifier, or the **EUDIW Intermediary Service** acting on its behalf, may consult the relevant status source to determine whether a credential or related trust object remains valid at the time of presentation.

In UC3, revocation is mainly relevant in cases where a credential should no longer be accepted before its natural expiry, for example because:

- it has been explicitly revoked by its issuer;
- its associated trust status has changed; or
- the pilot trust framework no longer recognises the relevant issuer or trust object as valid.

Because revocation is not a primary feature being piloted in UC3, the use case does not define complex episode-specific revocation behaviours. Instead, the assumption is that revocation is handled as part of the common verification controls applied during credential validation.

Accordingly, the UC3 approach is as follows:

- **issuance flows** provide the credential and any associated status information needed for later checking;
- **verification flows** may include a check of the relevant status or revocation source where this is supported by the pilot setup; and
- **revoked credentials or trust objects** are treated as invalid and should not be accepted by the relying party.

Overall, revocation in UC3 should therefore be understood as a **supporting trust and verification function**, implemented through the pilot’s adopted status-list / revocation mechanism where relevant, but not explored as a standalone or heavily featured pilot capability.
