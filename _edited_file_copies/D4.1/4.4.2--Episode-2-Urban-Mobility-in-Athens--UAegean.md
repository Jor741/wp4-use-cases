### Episode 2: Urban Mobility in Athens (UAegean)

Within the SEDIT-X use case, **Episode 2: Urban Mobility in Athens** extends the traveller journey beyond the airport and demonstrates how the **EUDI Wallet** can support a wallet-enabled payment interaction in a local mobility setting, in a manner that is consistent with the overall cross-domain logic of the pilot.

At scenario level, this episode contributes to the broader WP4 objective of showing that the EUDI Wallet can act as a **common interaction channel** across different service environments, allowing travellers to move from air transport to local mobility services without reverting to fragmented or repeatedly manual processes. In that sense, the urban mobility episode complements the airport, ferry, hospitality, and academic-access episodes by testing continuity of the wallet-enabled traveller experience across another operational domain.

At use-case level, the focus of the piloting is that, after arrival, the traveller uses the **EUDI Wallet to pay a participating taxi operator in Athens**. More specifically, Episode 2 is intended to pilot, in the urban mobility context, the **wallet-based payment flows that are being developed in WP6**. The traveller is therefore expected to interact with the taxi service through a wallet-supported payment process, where the EUDI Wallet is used to authorise the transaction and provide the payment-related evidence required for successful processing.

As with the other SEDIT-X episodes, the general implementation logic is that the traveller uses the wallet as the user-facing interaction channel, while the relevant service-side systems consume the verified result of the wallet transaction to support the operational decision. In this case, the operational outcome is the successful authorisation and completion of a taxi payment through the EUDI Wallet, demonstrating how SEDIT-X can connect mobility services with the broader wallet and payment ecosystem being developed across the project.

At the present stage of specification, the **exact piloting setup will depend on the taxi operator and technical environment available at pilot time**, as well as on the maturity of the WP6 payment implementation. For that reason, this episode should be understood as a **targeted pilot of wallet-based taxi payment in Athens**, rather than as a broader urban mobility access scenario.
