### UC 3: Seamless Digital Traveller Experience (SEDIT-X) (UAegean)

#### 7.6 Standards and Protocols

UC3 relies on a common EUDI Wallet interaction pattern across its transport, hospitality, and university-related episodes. At its core, the use case uses standard wallet-based **credential issuance** and **credential presentation/verification** flows, complemented by proximity interaction mechanisms, API-based integration with sectoral back-end systems, and, where relevant, payment and biometric subsystems.

The standards and protocols currently identified for WP4 implementation of UC3 are the following.

##### Core EUDI Wallet and Verifiable Credential Standards

- **eIDAS 2.0 / European Digital Identity Wallet framework**  
  UC3 is designed around the European Digital Identity Wallet ecosystem and its trust model, under which trusted issuers, wallets, verifiers, and intermediaries interact.

- **EUDI Wallet Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF)**  
  The ARF provides the architectural baseline for wallet roles, trust relationships, intermediary functions, credential exchange patterns, and conformity expectations across the participating domains.

- **Verifiable Credentials (VC)-based exchange model**  
  UC3 uses digitally issued and verifiable credentials as the baseline mechanism for representing identity, travel entitlement, booking references, academic/student status, disability-related attestations, and other domain-specific artefacts.

##### Issuance and Presentation Protocols

- **OpenID for Verifiable Credential Issuance (OpenID4VCI) 1.0**  
  This is the baseline issuance protocol used when credentials are delivered into the traveller’s wallet. In UC3, this applies for example to the issuance of:
  - boarding-pass-related credentials in the airport episode,
  - ferry boarding credentials in the ferry episode,
  - booking reference credentials in the hospitality episode,
  - and other domain credentials where wallet delivery is required.

- **OpenID for Verifiable Presentations (OpenID4VP) 1.0**  
  This is the baseline verification/presentation protocol used when a relying party requests credentials or attributes from the wallet. In UC3, it underpins presentation flows such as:
  - traveller identity and travel entitlement verification at airport touchpoints,
  - proximity-based ferry boarding validation,
  - booking-time identity support and self-service hotel check-in,
  - and student or entitlement verification in the university-related parts of the journey.

- **OAuth 2.0-based authorization patterns**  
  The issuance and presentation flows described for UC3 are based on OAuth-protected interactions, including authorization and token-based access where required by the issuance or verification profile.

##### Engagement and Device Interaction Patterns

- **QR code-based engagement**  
  QR-based initiation is used as one of the supported wallet engagement methods for presentation requests and user redirection to wallet actions.

- **Deep links / redirects / HTTPS-based wallet engagement**  
  UC3 assumes standard web-based or app-based engagement patterns for moving the user from the service environment to the wallet for consent and presentation.

- **Proximity interaction via NFC**  
  For proximity scenarios, especially in the airport and ferry episodes, UC3 foresees local device-to-device interaction where the traveller presents credentials from the wallet at the physical control point.

- **ISO/IEC 18013-5**  
  In the airport episode, the direct proximity verification path explicitly references unsupervised proximity verification via **ISO/IEC 18013-5**, as the mechanism for NFC-based presentation of identity- and travel-related evidence from the wallet.

- **DC API (where supported)**  
  The hotel flow notes that OpenID4VP may also be delivered through supported device engagement patterns such as **DC API**, where the participating environment supports it.

##### Intermediary and System Integration Layer

- **EUDIW Intermediary Service pattern**  
  UC3 uses an **EUDI Wallet Intermediary Service** as the main integration layer between wallet protocols and operational systems. This is particularly central in the airport, ferry, and hotel episodes, where the intermediary:
  - manages issuance and verification flows,
  - applies trust, validity, freshness, and revocation checks,
  - exposes APIs to booking, check-in, boarding, and hospitality systems,
  - and centralises operational logic instead of duplicating wallet handling at each touchpoint.

- **API-based integration with sectoral back-end systems**  
  UC3 assumes API-based integration between the intermediary and existing domain systems, such as:
  - airport and airline operational APIs (e.g. check-in, boarding eligibility, flight status, gate control, SSR-related data where needed),
  - booking, ticketing, and boarding systems in ferry transport,
  - hotel booking portals, DMC flows, PMS environments, and self-service terminals,
  - and university or other service-provider systems in downstream episodes.

##### Airport-Specific Technical Standards and Interaction Models

- **Biometric QR / checkpoint-readable token pattern**  
  One airport path uses a protected **biometric QR credential** as a checkpoint-readable journey token. This is not presented in the documents as a separate international standard, but as a pilot interaction model built on wallet issuance and verification logic.

- **1:1 biometric verification orchestration**  
  The airport episode includes optional **one-to-one biometric verification** at selected touchpoints. In this model, a live facial capture is compared against a reference associated with the presented credential or disclosed identity evidence, rather than against a central biometric database.

##### Payment-Related Standards and Protocols

- **TS12-aligned wallet payment authorization**  
  For scenarios involving wallet-mediated payments, UC3 refers to a **TS12-aligned** payment approach in which the wallet captures the user’s payment authorization and binds it to transaction details.

- **Strong User/Customer Authentication (SUA/SCA)**  
  The payment flow foresees the use of strong wallet-based user/customer authentication for authorizing the payment step.

- **Dynamic Linking**  
  The payment model referenced in the airport material includes **dynamic linking**, i.e. cryptographic binding of the user’s authorization to the concrete transaction context, such as amount and payee.

- **Propagation to card-based or account-to-account rails via PSP/payment interfaces**  
  After wallet authorization, the resulting evidence is forwarded through the relevant payment path, such as card-based payment gateway/PSP integrations or account-to-account interfaces.

##### Episode-Specific Notes

- **Airport (Episode 1):** most explicitly specified from a standards/protocol perspective; uses OpenID4VCI, OpenID4VP, ISO/IEC 18013-5 proximity interaction, QR-based engagement, API integration with airport/airline systems, and optional 1:1 biometric verification.
- **Ferry Transport (Episode 3):** follows the same wallet interaction logic as the airport and hotel domains, especially OpenID4VCI/OpenID4VP and proximity-style presentation, with operator-side ticketing and boarding integration.
- **Hospitality (Episode 4):** explicitly uses OpenID4VCI 1.0 for Booking Reference Credential issuance and OpenID4VP 1.0 for booking-time identity support and self-service hotel check-in.
- **Urban Mobility and University Access (Episodes 2 and 5):** the currently attached material is less prescriptive at protocol level, but these episodes are described as reusing the same common wallet issuance/presentation architecture and intermediary-based integration model.

##### Summary

In summary, UC3 is anchored in the **EUDI Wallet / ARF ecosystem**, with **OpenID4VCI 1.0** and **OpenID4VP 1.0** as the principal technical protocols for issuance and verification. These are complemented by **OAuth 2.0-based interactions**, **QR/deep-link/redirect engagement patterns**, **ISO/IEC 18013-5 proximity verification** for the airport NFC path, **API-based integration** with sectoral systems, and **TS12-aligned payment authorization** with **SCA/SUA** and **dynamic linking** where payment scenarios are piloted.
