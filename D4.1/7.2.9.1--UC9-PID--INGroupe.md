#### PID (Personal Identification Data)
Role of PID in the overall architecture: PID serves four core purposes:

* Identity proofing and high-assurance identification. PID provides the verified identity allowing the traveller to prove who they are.
* Binding of derived or domain-specific credentials. Travel and service credentials such as boarding passes or booking-reference credentials are operationally linked to the same traveller identity that is represented by the PID.
* Selective disclosure of identity attributes. The traveller presents only the attributes required for the transaction, rather than exposing the full credential content.
* Trust establishment across domains. PID enables a common identity layer across airport, ferry, hospitality, and university-related interactions.

* In order to properly identify the traveller, the following attributes will be verified from for their PID:
  - family_name
  - given_name
  - birth_date
  - nationality
