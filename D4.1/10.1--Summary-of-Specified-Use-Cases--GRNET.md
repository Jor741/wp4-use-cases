## Summary of Specified Use Cases

This section closes the deliverable with a short recap of the 11 use cases organised by scenario, and a summary of the key findings that the stock-taking phase surfaced. The detailed per-UC sheets are in **Annex A** and the cross-cutting mapping in @sec-4-1.

**Recap of the 11 use cases across the three scenarios**

The portfolio specified during T4.2 covers 11 use cases distributed across the three WP4 scenarios (Streamlined Travel, Hotel Check-In, Student Access), with several use cases spanning more than one scenario.

UC 3 (SEDIT-X) is the only use case that contributes to all three scenarios; UC 11 is the only use case that engages a non-EU credential-accepting party (Canada). Together, the 11 UCs exercise air, rail, ferry, bus, and urban transport; hotel check-in across multiple Member States; and student mobility within Europe and beyond.

**Key findings of the stock-taking phase**

The stock-taking phase has surfaced a small set of findings that concern the pilot portfolio itself, beyond the baseline findings about the today-state of the three sectors recorded in @sec-5.

* **Technical convergence is high.** All 11 UCs converge on the same small protocol set (OpenID4VCI for issuance, OpenID4VP for presentation, ISO/IEC 18013-5 for proximity, SD-JWT VC for remote), with no UC requiring a divergent stack. Shared tooling and shared testbed effort can therefore be reused across the portfolio.
* **The portfolio is genuinely cross-border.** Six of the 11 UCs (UC 3, UC 4, UC 6, UC 7, UC 8, UC 11) cross at least one Member State boundary in their main flow, and UC 11 even crosses the Union border into Canada. This is unusual for a pilot at this stage and exercises the cross-border recognition obligation of eIDAS 2.0 (@sec-6-1) more directly than a single-MS pilot would.
