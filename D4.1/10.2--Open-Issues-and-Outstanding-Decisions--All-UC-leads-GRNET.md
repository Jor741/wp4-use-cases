## Open Issues

### UC 1 Board the Flight 

- **Digital credential maturity & legal validity**
  - Legal acceptance of **PID** and **DTC** for boarding is still uneven across travel contexts.
  - Decisions depend on local regulation and authority acceptance, not only technical readiness.


- **Intra‑Schengen inconsistency**
  - No systematic identity collection online by default.
  - Identity checks may still be required at departure at certain airports.
  - Leads to fragmented passenger experience and unclear credential strategy.

- **Real‑world piloting constraints**
  - Meaningful testing requires **real passengers, real airlines, and real airports**.
  - Implies **heavy legal, regulatory, and liability reviews** before any pilot.
  - Limits speed and scope of experimentation compared to pure demos or simulations.
  
### SEDIT-X Use case
The SEDIT-X use case is already sufficiently defined to support ongoing specification and technical preparation. At the same time, several open issues and outstanding decisions remain, mainly because the pilot spans multiple domains, depends on external partners, and includes elements whose final implementation depends on the maturity of the participating technical environments.

The main open issues for UC3 are summarised below.

#### Level of technical integration at each participating environment

The final degree of integration with sector-specific systems still needs to be decided. In some cases, the pilot may support a more automated and API-based flow, while in other cases a lighter or partly assisted model may be more realistic.

 
#### Payment-flow implementation details

For the payment-related parts of SEDIT-X, notably airport excess-baggage payments and ferry-ticket payments, the exact operational flow still needs to be confirmed together with the relevant payment partners and the maturity of the WP6 building blocks.
  

#### Hospitality deployment variants

For the hotel episode, the participating hotel environments may support different implementation models, ranging from reception-assisted verification to more integrated self-service flows. A decision is still needed on which deployment variants will actually be piloted.

#### Academic-access service scope

For the academic-access episode, the final set of services to be exposed through the wallet still needs to be confirmed. This includes deciding whether the pilot will focus mainly on physical access, digital-service access, or a combination of both.

#### Success criteria for end-to-end piloting

A final decision is also needed on what should count as sufficient pilot success at UC3 level. Given the breadth of SEDIT-X, the project may need to confirm whether success means full end-to-end continuity across all episodes, or successful piloting of each episode under a common architectural approach.


### UC 4
The UC4 scope is defined, but a few things are still open on the implementation side. The main one is whether DTC derived and airport issued access credentials will actually be accepted in real airport environments, since that depends on regulation and what each airport is willing to support. The way PID, DTC and the final access credential are linked also still needs to be properly fixed, so the identity flow stays consistent end to end. On the technical side, it’s not yet decided how far the integration at airport access points will go, meaning whether everything runs fully API-based in real time. Lastly, biometrics as an optional step still needs some clarity, especially around consent and how that is actually handled inside the wallet flow.


### UC 5
The UC5 scope is already aligned with a live environment, but there are still a few open points on the implementation side while moving toward EUDI / ARF compliance.

The main open question is how the existing live FastID setup will be adapted to fully align with ARF, OpenID4VC and SD-JWT based credential flows without changing the current user experience at stadium entry. Since the system is already in production, changes need to be introduced gradually and validated against the existing flow.

Another open poiint is how the linkage between PID, DTC and the stadium access credential is formalised in a fully standardised way, while still keeping compatibility with the current live issuance logic used by the football club.

It is also still open how far biometric verification will remain part of the flow in the long term, especially in relation to consent handling and whether it is always required or only used in specific access scenarios.

Finally, there is an open question around student-related and intern-related access, mainly whether these entitlements should remain embedded in the current access credential logic or be expressed through separate attribute attestations in future iterations.


### UC6
--------
UC6 aims to define the proper Student Card data model, in order to ensure status verification and service access for students in compliance with eIDAS standards and actual university framework. European Student Card will serve as a base for the main data but it will be defined according to the actual processes. 

The primary focus of UC6 is the definition of a robust **Student Card data model** that bridges the gap between current university frameworks and the new eIDAS 2.0 standards. While the **European Student Card (ESC)** provides the foundational data structure, several open questions remain regarding its technical and operational evolution:
- **Data Model:** a key outstanding issue is the final alignment of the ESC data model with the specific attributes required by diverse service providers (e.g., transport). The project must still define how to balance actual university processes with the strict credentials' technical requirements.
- **Lifecycle:** beyond the pilot phase, it remains to be determined how the credential lifecycle will be managed at scale. Future directions include exploring automated synchronization between university enrollment databases and the EUDI Wallet to ensure real-time status accuracy.
- **Additional services:** while the current scope focuses on specific student benefits, there is an open question regarding the integration with other services to allow the student card to be used daily.


### UC7-8
-------
For **UC 7 and UC 8**, the pilot boundaries are well-defined, and the testing infrastructure is currently being finalized. However, the transition from a controlled test environment to a production-ready ecosystem presents several outstanding issues:
- **Pre-production system finalization:** a major issue remains the finalization of **Trenitalia’s pre-production system**. While the PagoPA test app provides a stable environment for wallet-side testing, Trenitalia must complete the integration of its backend to ensure full compliance with the Aptitude framework and EUDIW standards.
- **Cross-border journeys:** UC8 requires specific fares for the pilot, since the double identity verification is restricted to certain city connections (e.g. Rome-Monaco).
- **Multi-modal Integration:** a future direction involves expanding the verification logic to include other transport modes (bus, metro, air), moving toward a truly integrated travel experience where the PID and eligibility attributes (like the Disability Card) are recognized across the entire European transport network.

  

### UC 9
The UC9 scope and limits are currently well-defined with dependencies identified and limitation in place. Adjustments might still need to be made at implementation especially regarding the number to airport verification touchpoints.
