#### Trust Framework
## Trusted Issuer Lists — Amadeus *Board the Flight*

### Purpose

In the *Amadeus Board the Flight* use case, relying parties must be able to determine **whether the issuer of a presented credential is trusted** for the purposes of boarding‑related verification.

The **Trusted Issuer List** provides the issuer‑recognition component of the trust framework.  
It answers one specific question:

> *Is this issuer trusted to issue this type of credential for the Board the Flight use case?*

### Target Production Model

In the **target production environment**, trusted issuer recognition is expected to rely on the **eIDAS 2.0 / EUDI Wallet ecosystem**, where:
- issuers are recognised through shared European trust infrastructures;
- relying parties do not rely on bilateral trust agreements; and
- issuer authorisation is transparent and interoperable across Member States.

### APTITUDE Pilot Trusted Issuer List

For the **APTITUDE pilot**, trusted issuer recognition is implemented via an **APTITUDE pilot Trusted Issuer List (TIL)**.

This pilot list:
- mirrors the function of an eIDAS‑style trust list;
- is limited to pilot‑authorised issuers; and
- is used exclusively within the scope of the pilot.

The Trusted Issuer List can be consulted directly by Amadeus systems or via an **EUDIW Intermediary Service** acting on Amadeus’s behalf.

### Role of the Trusted Issuer List

The Trusted Issuer List:

- supports **issuer recognition**;
- does **not** replace signature validation, credential validation, or status checks; and
- acts as a **precondition** for further verification steps.

Only credentials issued by an issuer recognised in the Trusted Issuer List, and authorised for the relevant credential type, can proceed to full verification.

### Issuer Categories Relevant to *Board the Flight*

For the *Board the Flight* use case, the Trusted Issuer List is expected to support at least the following issuer categories:

- **PID issuers / national identity authorities**
- **Transport‑related issuers**, such as airlines issuing boarding passes or travel entitlements
- **Other authorised attestation providers** explicitly participating in the pilot and relevant to boarding decisions

### Minimum Information per Issuer

For each trusted issuer, the list should enable Amadeus to determine:

- the **issuer identity**;
- the **trust context** under which the issuer is recognised;
- the **credential types and roles** the issuer is authorised to provide for Board the Flight; and
- the **issuer status** (e.g. active, suspended, expired in pilot context).

### Use in the Verification Flow

When a credential is presented in *Board the Flight*:

1. Amadeus (or the EUDIW Intermediary Service) checks whether the issuer is listed in the **Trusted Issuer List**.
2. It verifies that the issuer is authorised for the **presented credential type**.
3. If issuer recognition succeeds, Amadeus proceeds with:
   - cryptographic verification,
   - validity and status checks, and
   - operational eligibility checks.

If issuer recognition fails, the credential is rejected without proceeding further.

### Summary

For *Amadeus Board the Flight*, the Trusted Issuer List:

- acts as the **issuer‑recognition layer** of the trust framework;
- enables **cross‑border issuer recognition** without bilateral configuration; and
- supports realistic piloting while remaining aligned with the future eIDAS 2.0 / EUDI Wallet ecosystem.

It defines **who is trusted to issue what**, while the broader trust framework determines **whether a specific credential presentation should be accepted**.
