## UC 4: Streamlined Travel Experience (FastID)

The FastID use case sit within the Streamlined Travel Experience scenario and looks at how a DTC, derived from a verified identity in the EUDI Wallet, can be used together with an access credential to support access to controlled airport areas for both visitors and employees. The focus in WP4 is on making airport access flows work in a way where identity verification, credential issuance and physical entry fit together without adding unnecesary friction. The implementation is based on a Schiphol Lab environment, which acts as a controlled setup reflecting airport-like access conditions rather than a live operational system.

The flow starts with the user onboarding their identity in the FastID wallet through a check on their identity document, which results in a PID-based identity. From there, a DTC is generated and linked to the user’s identity attributes. After that, an access credential is issued into the wallet, representing the permission to access specific airport areas and being tied to both the identity and the DTC. In practice, both credentials are used together when access is requested.

At the access point, the user presents the credential via the wallet, typically through a QR-based flow. The system checks validity and if everything matches the expected identity context, wchich then grants access. The same approach applies to both visitors and employees, depending on the rights that have been assigned, with the main idea being that one wallet interaction replaces multiple manual checks.

From a scenario point of view, UC 4 shows how wallet identity, travel credentials and access rights can be combined to streamline airport access in a way that stays interoperable and works across different user types and contexts.
