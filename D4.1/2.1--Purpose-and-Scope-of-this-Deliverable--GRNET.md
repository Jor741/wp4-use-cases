## Purpose and Scope of this Deliverable

### Purpose

Deliverable D4.1, titled *UC Specifications and Scenarios*, is the primary outcome of Task T4.2 (Stock-taking and Specifications) within Work Package 4 of the APTITUDE project. Its purpose is to provide a comprehensive and structured account of the use cases that WP4 will pilot, together with the surrounding analysis that informed their design.

D4.1 serves multiple audiences. For the **European Commission**, it demonstrates that the APTITUDE consortium has completed the stock-taking phase, producing specifications that are grounded in real operational needs and aligned with the regulatory framework established by eIDAS 2.0 and its Implementing Acts. For **UC leads and implementing partners**, it provides the authoritative reference against which implementation work in Task T4.3 will be carried out. For **other Work Packages** within APTITUDE, particularly WP2 (Technical Standards), WP3 (Digital Travel Credentials), and WP6 (Payment and Banking), it documents the interfaces and dependencies that require coordination. Finally, for **other Large Scale Pilots** and the broader EUDIW ecosystem, it offers a detailed view of how the wallet can be applied to tickets and check-in scenarios across transport, hospitality, and student mobility.

### What is in scope

This deliverable covers the following areas:

* **Use case specifications.** Each of the 11 accepted WP4 use cases is specified following a standardised template. The specifications include a summary description, user stories, actor definitions, context and preconditions, the credentials involved, step-by-step user journeys, technical flows with accompanying diagrams, and unhappy paths. These specifications are the core content of the deliverable, presented in Chapter 8 and detailed further in the Annex A specification sheets.
* **Business requirements.** For each use case, the deliverable captures the business-level requirements that drive the need for wallet-based solutions: what the current process looks like, where it falls short, and what the EUDIW-enabled process should achieve. These requirements emerge from the baseline analysis presented in Chapter 4.
* **Legal and regulatory requirements.** The deliverable analyses the legal context that constrains or enables the WP4 use cases. This includes the provisions of the eIDAS 2.0 Regulation and its Implementing Acts, as well as sector-specific regulations covering hotel guest registration, transport and passenger rights, and student mobility. This analysis is presented in Chapter 5.
* **Solution design.** The deliverable describes the common technical foundations that underpin all WP4 use cases, including EUDIW architecture alignment, credential types and data models, issuance and verification flows, selective disclosure mechanisms, and the trust framework. These elements are presented in Chapter 6, while technical details for each UC are included in the individual specification sheets.
* **Interoperability requirements.** Both functional and non-functional interoperability requirements are addressed. Functional requirements cover what the wallet, credential issuers, and relying parties should be able to do (e.g., issue a boarding pass into the wallet, verify a PID at a hotel kiosk). Non-functional requirements cover the quality attributes that the implementations must exhibit, such as offline capability for ferry boarding, and accessibility for users with reduced mobility. Finally, cross-border interoperability, which is a core characteristic of the APTITUDE pilot, receives particular attention throughout the deliverable.

### What is out of scope

Certain topics are deliberately excluded from D4.1, as they belong to subsequent phases of the WP4 work plan:

* **Implementation details** are out of scope. The technical architecture decisions, software component designs, API specifications, deployment configurations, and integration guides that implementing partners will need are the subject of Deliverable D4.2 (Implementation Manual), which is the output of Task T4.3 (Implementation Support). D4.1 specifies *what* must be implemented and *why*, while D4.2 will specify *how*.
* **Piloting results and validation** are also out of scope. The execution of pilot tests, the collection of user feedback, the measurement of Key Performance Indicators, and the evaluation of whether the use cases met their objectives are the subject of Deliverable D4.3 (Validation Report), which is the output of Task T4.4 (Piloting, User Testing, and UC Evaluation). D4.1 establishes the baseline and the specifications against which D4.3 will later measure success.
* **Wallet development** is out of scope. WP4 does not develop the EUDIW itself; it consumes wallet instances provided by Member State wallet issuers. The wallet's internal architecture, security mechanisms, and certification are addressed by other parts of the APTITUDE project and by the MS national wallet programmes.

### Relationship between D4.1, D4.2, and D4.3

The three WP4 deliverables form a deliberate progression:

1. **D4.1 (this document)** answers the question: *What are we building and why?* It provides the specifications, the legal grounding, and the baseline analysis.
2. **D4.2 (Implementation Manual)** will answer the question: *How do we build it?* It will translate the D4.1 specifications into concrete implementation guidance, covering technical integration, testing procedures, and deployment instructions.
3. **D4.3 (Validation Report)** will answer the question: *Did it work?* It will report on the pilot execution, presenting test results, user feedback, and an evaluation of each use case against its stated objectives and KPIs.

This progression ensures that the pilot activities are traceable from requirements (D4.1) through implementation (D4.2) to validation (D4.3), providing a clear audit trail for the European Commission and for future EUDIW deployments that may build on the APTITUDE experience.
