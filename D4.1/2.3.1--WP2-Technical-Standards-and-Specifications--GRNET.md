### WP2 — Technical Standards and Specifications

**WP2** is the technical backbone of APTITUDE. Among its goals are to define the implementation profiles, operate the interoperability testbed, and provide the trust infrastructure on which all use case Work Packages converge. WP4 implementations must conform to WP2 outputs, and WP4 consumes WP2 profiles and reports conformance through the WP2 testbed.

**WP2 deliverables that WP4 consumes**

There are four WP2 deliverables connected with WP4:

* **D2.1 — Technical Implementation Profiles.** The selection and profiling of the standards on which WP4 implementations must align (see the consolidated standards list below).
* **D2.2 — Testbed Report** and **D2.3 — Project Infrastructural Services.** The interoperability testbed and supporting services on which every WP4 use case is validated for conformance before the pilot phase.
* **D2.4 — Guidelines and Best Practices.** The cross-WP guidance to which WP4 implementation work in T4.3 will refer.

**The consolidated standards list**

The standards that WP4 references were recorded by all UC leads in Nextcloud. The per-UC entries captured for each entry, the issuing organisation, the version, the publication status (Final, Draft, Adopted), and a coded assessment of the entry's applicability to credential issuance, issuance protocol, presentation protocol, credential format, trust models, and legal value.

The entries fall into the following groups:

* **EUDIW core (eIDAS and ARF).** eIDAS Regulation including eIDAS 2.0 (Final), and the **EUDI Wallet Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF)** at version 2.X (Draft). The ARF is applicable to issuance, issuance protocol, presentation protocol, credential format, trust models, and legal value, with privacy-by-design, selective disclosure, unlinkability, and wallet-governance properties recorded explicitly.
* **ETSI trust framework standards.** ETSI TS 119 461 v2.1.1 (identity proofing), ETSI TS 119 471 v1.1.1 (QTSP QEAA policies), ETSI TS 119 472-1 v1.1.1 (EAA format, issuance, presentation profiles), ETSI TS 119 478 v1.1.1 (authentic sources), and ETSI TS 119 612 (Trust Lists / Status, latest). These standards underpin the trust framework consumed by WP4 in @sec-7_5.
* **ISO/IEC proximity and online mDL standards.** ISO/IEC 18013-5 (2021, Final) for proximity verification, and ISO/IEC 18013-7 (2024, Final) for online and remote mDL presentation.
* **OpenID Foundation issuance and presentation profiles.** OIDC for Verifiable Credential Issuance (OIDC4VCI) v1.0 Final, OIDC for Verifiable Presentations (OIDC4VP) v1.0 Final, and OPENID4VC-HAIP (High Assurance Profile, Draft 6/1). HAIP is the assurance profile that applies to the WP4 high-assurance flows.
* **IETF specifications.** SD-JWT v1.0 (Final), SD-JWT VC (IETF draft), OAUTH-ATTESTATION (Draft 7), OAUTH-MULT-RESP (Final, response encoding), RFC 5280 (PKI and certificates, Final), and TOKEN-STATUS-LIST (Draft 13, revocation and status). These specifications collectively cover the credential format and the credential lifecycle plumbing that WP4 implementations sit on top of.
* **W3C.** Verifiable Credentials Data Model 2.0 (Final), used as the conceptual reference for credential semantics alongside the concrete serialisations.
* **Legal instruments referenced as technical-impact items.** GDPR (Reg. (EU) 2016/679), GDPR Article 9 on biometric data, PSD2/PSD3 for payments, the AI Act for biometric provisions, and the European Student Card Initiative (ESCI). These are also recorded in @sec-6 from the legal-analysis perspective; they appear in the standards list because they constrain the technical design.
* **Sector-specific and partner-specific entries.** These entries fall outside the EUDIW core standards baseline and reflect specific partner stacks that WP4 must interface with at the touchpoints where they appear, rather than standards that apply across the portfolio.

The detailed per-UC standards selection is in @sec-7_6_1 through @sec-7_6_11.

**WP2 implementation profiles**

WP2 organises its implementation profiles in two layers, both published on the project's GitHub repository (`github.com/APTITUDE-Consortium/aptitude-eudi-wallet-specs`). Layer 1 contains the **horizontal RFCs** shared across all piloting Work Packages: **RFC-001 (Credential Issuance Profile)** and **RFC-002 (Presentation Profile)** are already published, while **RFC-003 (Trust Framework, building on ETSI TS 119 612 with LoTE structures mapped to ETSI TS 119 602)** and **RFC-004 (Revocation, via Status List Tokens in JWT for WRPRC and CRL and/or OCSP for WRPAC)** are in drafting at the time of D4.1. Layer 2 contains the **vertical, credential-specific rulebooks and RFCs**, one per piloting Work Package; for WP4 this is the **Tickets & Check-in Rulebook** and its companion **vertical RFC**, each of which covers attributes and metadata, encoding format, issuance flow, presentation flow, verification flow, and trust and revocation.
