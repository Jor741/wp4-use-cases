### UC 6: European Student Experience/Student Access via EUDI Wallet (iDAKTO-PagoPA)

**WP4-UC6** implementation aligns with the EUDIW ARF by leveraging its core roles and components:
- The **wallet instance** is used by students to store and present their student credentials. PagoPA and ANTS will provide the EUDIW, using mock data.    
- The **PID provider** ensures the initial binding of the wallet to a verified identity, enabling trust in the credential holder. 
- The **attestation provider** (e.g. university or authorised issuer) is responsible for issuing the student credential (student status or digital student card) to the wallet.
- Finally, **relying parties** (e.g. transport providers, cultural institutions, accommodation services) request and verify the credential to grant access to services or benefits.
  
The implementation follows the protocol profiles defined in the **ARF for credential issuance and presentation**, ensuring secure, interoperable exchanges between wallet, issuers, and relying parties. This could include support for selective disclosure, allowing students to prove their status without sharing unnecessary personal data.

The baseline version used is aligned with the latest available ARF specifications applicable to the project, ensuring consistency with ongoing developments under eIDAS 2.0 and compatibility with other use cases within WP4.
