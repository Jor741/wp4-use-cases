### UC 1: Board the flight (Amadeus)


The **Board the Flight** use case is designed to align with the role model and interaction patterns defined in the **European Digital Identity Wallet Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF)**.  
It applies the standard **EUDI Wallet relationship** between **issuer**, **wallet/holder**, and **verifier / relying party** across the **online check‑in** and **airport boarding** phases of the passenger journey.

This use case does **not introduce a separate or proprietary architecture**. Instead, it demonstrates how the **ARF model can be applied in an aviation context**, using **Digital Travel Credentials (DTC)** issued to and presented from the **EUDI Wallet (EUDIW)**, in support of **Scenario 1: Streamlined Travel Experience**.

---

### ARF baseline used for UC1 – Board the Flight

For the purposes of **UC1 – Board the Flight**, the architectural baseline is the **EUDI Wallet Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF) v2.8.0**.  

Compared to earlier ARF iterations (v1.x and early v2.x), **ARF v2.8.0 represents a consolidation and maturation step**, particularly for **person identity (PID)**, **Photo ID‑based attestations**, and **travel‑related credentials such as DTC**



**ARF v2.8.0 – Key improvements**
- **Clearer positioning of domain‑specific credentials**, including DTC, as:
  - first‑class, wallet‑native attestations
  - issued by trusted sector issuers
- Stronger emphasis on:
  - **credential reuse across contexts**
    (online → physical, remote → proximity)
  - trust checks, status checks, and freshness
- Better conceptual alignment with:
  - **ICAO Digital Travel Credential (DTC) developments**
  - **IATA One ID principles**
- More explicit treatment of **intermediaries** as facilitators for DTC issuance and verification.

**Impact for Board the Flight**
- Supports a **single DTC issued at online check‑in** and:
  - reused at the airport for boarding
- Enables a **wallet‑centric travel experience**, without:
  - physical boarding passes
  - repeated document presentation

This version defines the reference role model for:

- Wallet Units / Wallet Instances  
- PID Providers  
- Attestation Providers  
- Relying Parties / Verifiers  
- Intermediaries  

The ARF frames the ecosystem as one in which users obtain **attestations from trusted issuers** and present them to **relying parties** in a **privacy‑preserving and user‑controlled manner**.

This baseline is used as the reference point for mapping all UC1 roles and interaction flows. While the ARF is expected to evolve further through implementing acts and future revisions, the **Board the Flight** use case is **already structurally aligned** with the **v2.8.0 role model and interaction logic**.

### ARF components used in UC1 – Board the Flight

#### Wallet Instance / Wallet Unit

UC1 assumes that the traveler uses a conformant **EUDI Wallet instance** on a personal device under the traveler’s control.  
The wallet:

- stores the **PID** and the **Digital Travel Credential (DTC)** used for air travel;
- authenticates the user where required by the issuance or presentation profile;
- supports **explicit** ( e.g tap your smart phone is a way to explicitly express this contextual affirmative action, rather than a seperate onsent form) user consent; and
- constructs verifiable presentations for disclosure to airline and airport systems.


In a proximity flow (e.g. boarding gate), the user interaction is intentionally minimal. Usability constraints are not optional – they are operational necessities.

The wallet itself is **not implemented by the use case**. UC1 consumes wallet instances provided by the broader **EUDI Wallet ecosystem**.

In UC1, the wallet is the **single, consistent user interaction channel**, reused across online and airport touchpoints.

---



#### PID or DTC Issuer

In UC1, the **Person Identification Data(PID) or Digital Travel Credential (DTC) Issuer role** is primarily fulfilled by the **authorities**.  

This role issues **Person Identification Data(PID) or Digital Travel Credential (DTC)** to the traveler’s EUDI Wallet as **pre-requisite** of UC1 

This issuance process must happen outside the UC1 , e.g outside  online check‑in phase. 

The Issuer is the legally accountable source of the attested data, while the ARF Attestation Provider is the technical role that issues and delivers the attestation to the wallet in compliance with the ARF.

- **Issuer**: the legal role responsible for issuing a credential or attestation.  
- **ARF Attestation Provider**: a technical and operational role defined by the eIDAS 2.0 Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF) that performs the issuance process toward the wallet.

👉 In practice, these roles may be fulfilled by the same entity or by two different entities.


---

#### Relying Party / Verifier

The **Relying Party / Verifier** role is played by:

- airline systems during **online check‑in**; and
- airline and airport operational systems at the **boarding gate**.

These systems:
- prepare presentation requests;
- receive verifiable presentations from the wallet;
- validate trust, integrity, credential status, and freshness; and
- return an operational decision such as *boarding allowed*.
