# Annex C: Glossary

This annex presents all terms, concepts, standards, protocols, regulatory instruments, organisations, and acronyms used throughout deliverable D4.1. It expands the key definitions introduced in @sec-2_5 and provides a single reference point for readers moving between the main chapters and the Use Case specification sheets in Annex A.

Entries are organised into six sections, within each section, entries are listed alphabetically.

## Acronyms and Abbreviations

**[AI Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)** — The EU Artificial Intelligence Act, Regulation (EU) 2024/1689. Introduces harmonised rules for AI systems, including specific constraints on biometric identification systems. Relevant for UC 9 and SEDIT-X Episode 1.

**AML / KYC** — Anti-Money-Laundering / Know-Your-Customer. Bodies of obligation that apply at the payment touchpoints of WP4 use cases coordinated with WP6.

**[API](https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/TRIP/Publications/WCO-IATA-ICAO-API-Guidelines.pdf)** *(border-control sense)* — Advance Passenger Information. Passenger-and-document data that airlines must transmit to border authorities prior to inbound flights, as governed by Regulation (EU) 2025/12 and its predecessors.

**API** *(software development)* — Application Programming Interface. The general meaning is also used in the deliverable when describing wallet, intermediary, and back-office integrations.

**[APIS](https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/TRIP/Publications/WCO-IATA-ICAO-API-Guidelines.pdf)** — Advance Passenger Information System. The carrier-to-authority transmission regime for API data.

**APTITUDE** — The Large Scale Pilot project under which D4.1 is produced. Pilots ticket and check-in use cases using the EUDI Wallet across transport, hospitality, and student-mobility sectors.

**[ARF](https://eu-digital-identity-wallet.github.io/eudi-doc-architecture-and-reference-framework/)** — Architecture and Reference Framework. See *Architecture and Reference Framework* in Section 2.

**[BLE](https://www.bluetooth.com/specifications/specs/)** — Bluetooth Low Energy. A short-range radio technology used at proximity touchpoints (e.g. door reader in UC 10).

**[BOE](https://www.boe.es/)** — *Boletín Oficial del Estado*. The Spanish official journal in which national civil-aviation security measures are published (e.g. BOE-A-2021-14195).

**[CAQ](https://www.quebec.ca/en/immigration/students/quebec-acceptance-certificate)** — *Certificat d'acceptation du Québec*. Quebec provincial acceptance certificate required for studies in Quebec, issued by MIFI. Referenced in UC 11.

**[CBOR](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc8949)** — Concise Binary Object Representation. The binary encoding underlying the mdoc credential format (ISO/IEC 18013-5).

**[DC API](https://www.w3.org/TR/digital-credentials/)** — Digital Credentials API. See Section 4.

**[DC4EU](https://www.dc4eu.eu/)** — Digital Credentials for Europe. A prior EU Large Scale Pilot referenced as a potential consultation source in the stock-taking phase.

**[DG1 / DG2](https://www.icao.int/publications/pages/publication.aspx?docnum=9303)** — Data Group 1 / Data Group 2. ICAO-defined data structures within travel documents (and their digital counterparts). DG1 carries biographic / MRZ-derived attributes; DG2 carries the biometric reference (typically a facial image).

**[DIACC](https://diacc.ca/)** — Digital ID and Authentication Council of Canada. Coordinator on the Canadian side for UC 11; maintainer of the Pan-Canadian Trust Framework.

**[DPIA](https://gdpr-info.eu/art-35-gdpr/)** — Data Protection Impact Assessment. Required by GDPR Article 35 for high-risk processing such as biometric identification. Coordinated under WP7.

**[DTC](https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/TRIP/Publications/High-Level-Guidance-explaining-ICAO-DTC.pdf)** — Digital Travel Credential. See Section 3.

**EAA** — Electronic Attestation of Attributes. See Section 3.

**[EDPB](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/)** — European Data Protection Board. Issued Opinion 11/2024 on facial-recognition use to streamline airport passenger flows.

**[EES](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2017/2226/oj)** — Entry/Exit System. Centralised EU system established by Regulation (EU) 2017/2226 for recording entries and exits of third-country nationals at Schengen external borders.

**[eIDAS / eIDAS 2.0](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1183/oj)** — *electronic IDentification, Authentication and trust Services*. The EU regulatory framework for digital identity and trust services; "eIDAS 2.0" denotes the major revision in Regulation (EU) 2024/1183 introducing the EUDIW.

**[eMRTD](https://www.icao.int/publications/pages/publication.aspx?docnum=9303)** — electronic Machine Readable Travel Document. The chip-enabled passport family standardised by ICAO Doc 9303.

**[ERASMUS / Erasmus+](https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/)** — The EU programme for student mobility and cooperation in education, governed by Regulation (EU) 2021/817.

**[ERUA](https://erua-eui.eu/)** — European Reform University Alliance. A European Universities alliance, of which the University of the Aegean is a member; relevant for SEDIT-X Episode 5 and UC 6.

**[ESCI](https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/european-student-card-initiative)** — European Student Card Initiative. EU initiative providing the reference framework for student credentials, including the European Student Card and Erasmus Without Paper (EWP) services.

**ETA** — Electronic Travel Authorization. A pre-travel screening permit issued by destination countries (e.g. UK ETA, Canadian eTA). UC 2 generalises this concept.

**[ETIAS](https://travel-europe.europa.eu/etias_en)** — European Travel Information and Authorisation System. Regulation (EU) 2018/1240; the EU's pre-travel screening regime for visa-exempt third-country nationals.

**[EUDIW / EUDI Wallet](https://eu-digital-identity-wallet.github.io/eudi-doc-architecture-and-reference-framework/)** — European Digital Identity Wallet. See Section 2.

**[EURAXESS](https://euraxess.ec.europa.eu/)** — European researcher mobility network referenced in Scenario 3 (Student Access).

**EWC** — European Wallet Consortium. Prior pilot project where Board the Flight (UC 1) was previously tested with mock data.

**[EWP](https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/european-student-card-initiative/ewp)** — Erasmus Without Paper. Digital infrastructure for the exchange of student-mobility data between European higher-education institutions.

**[GDPR](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj)** — General Data Protection Regulation, Regulation (EU) 2016/679. See Section 5.

**GYSC** — Generic Youth Student Credential issuer (used in the deliverable as the issuer of the French student EAA in UC 11; also referenced for UC 6 alongside ISIC).

**[HTTPS](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc9110)** — HyperText Transfer Protocol Secure. Transport channel used for SD-JWT VC remote flows.

**[iAPI](https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/TRIP/Publications/WCO-IATA-ICAO-API-Guidelines.pdf)** — Interactive Advance Passenger Information. Real-time API data exchange complementing the static API regime.

**[IATA](https://www.iata.org/)** — International Air Transport Association. Source of the One ID initiative and aviation-industry guidance referenced for UC 1, UC 4, UC 9, and SEDIT-X Episode 1.

**[ICAO](https://www.icao.int/)** — International Civil Aviation Organization. Standard-setting body for travel documents (Doc 9303), Digital Travel Credentials, and Annex 9 (Facilitation).

**[IPZS](https://www.ipzs.it/)** — *Istituto Poligrafico e Zecca dello Stato*. The Italian State Mint and Polygraphic Institute; official Italian PID and credential issuer for UC 6, UC 7, and UC 8.

**[IRCC](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship.html)** — Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada. Canadian federal authority responsible for study-permit issuance, referenced in UC 11.

**[ISIC](https://www.isic.org/)** — International Student Identity Card. International student credential referenced alongside GYSC for SEDIT-X Episode 5 and UC 6.

**[IT Wallet](https://italia.github.io/eid-wallet-it-docs/)** — The Italian national public digital wallet operated by PagoPA; the production counterpart of the PagoPA EUDIW test app.

**KPI** — Key Performance Indicator. Used in UC specifications to define D4.3 success criteria.

**LoA** — Level of Assurance. eIDAS-defined assurance levels for electronic identification means; "high" is the level required for the PID.

**LSP** — Large Scale Pilot. The category of EU-funded projects under which APTITUDE operates.

**[mdoc](https://www.iso.org/standard/69084.html)** — mobile document. See Section 3.

**[mDL](https://www.iso.org/standard/69084.html)** — mobile Driving Licence. The mobile-document use case originally standardised by ISO/IEC 18013-5; cited as the prototypical mdoc.

**[MRTD](https://www.icao.int/publications/pages/publication.aspx?docnum=9303)** — Machine Readable Travel Document. ICAO-standardised travel document family; superset that includes paper passports, eMRTDs, and DTCs.

**[MRZ](https://www.icao.int/publications/pages/publication.aspx?docnum=9303)** — Machine Readable Zone. The OCR-readable section of an MRTD from which DG1 attributes are derived.

**mVRC** — mobile Vehicle Registration Credential. Wallet-held attestation about a vehicle and its registration, used in SEDIT-X Episode 3 for ferry-with-vehicle flows.

**[NFC](https://nfc-forum.org/)** — Near Field Communication. Short-range radio used for wallet–reader proximity exchanges (e.g. door reader in UC 10, gate reader in SEDIT-X Episode 1).

**[NIS2](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2022/2555/oj)** — Network and Information Security Directive 2, Directive (EU) 2022/2555. Sets cybersecurity risk-management and incident-reporting obligations on essential and important entities.

**[NOBID](https://www.nobidconsortium.com/)** — Nordic-Baltic eID Project. Prior EU LSP referenced as a potential source of consultation.

**[OAuth 2.0](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc6749)** — IETF authorisation framework on which OpenID4VCI and OpenID4VP are built.

**[OID4VP / OpenID4VP](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-presentations-1_0.html)** — OpenID for Verifiable Presentations. See Section 4.

**[OIDC4VCI / OpenID4VCI](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-credential-issuance-1_0.html)** — OpenID for Verifiable Credential Issuance. See Section 4.

**[OSDM](https://osdm.io/)** — Open Sales and Distribution Model. Rail-industry data model referenced as the issuer context for digital train tickets in WP4.

**OTA** — Online Travel Agency. Channel through which many hotel bookings are originated today; relevant baseline for UC 10 and SEDIT-X Episode 4.

**PBEE** — Payment and Banking EUDIW Extension. The framework being developed by WP6 to standardise wallet-based payment features.

**[PCTF](https://diacc.ca/trust-framework/)** — Pan-Canadian Trust Framework. The Canadian trust framework maintained by DIACC; relevant for UC 11.

**[PhotoID](https://www.iso.org/standard/74910.html)** — Mobile eID-style identity attestation defined under ISO/IEC 23220, used in WP3 / UC 9 alongside or in lieu of the PID for biometric profile issuance.

**[PID](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2977/oj)** — Person Identification Data (also written *Personal Identification Data*). See Section 3.

**PIN** — Personal Identification Number. Local wallet authentication factor used together with biometrics for user consent at presentation time.

**[Polimi](https://www.polimi.it/)** — Politecnico di Milano. Issuer of the Italian student card for UC 6.

**PMS** — Property Management System. Hotel back-office system that records reservations, check-in/-out, and guest details; integration target in UC 10 and SEDIT-X Episode 4.

**POTENTIAL** — Prior EU Large Scale Pilot referenced as a potential source of consultation.

**[PSD2](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2015/2366/oj)** — Payment Services Directive 2, Directive (EU) 2015/2366. Establishes Strong Customer Authentication obligations and PSP rules.

**PSP** — Payment Service Provider. Counterparty in WP6-mediated payment flows; mentioned in the SEDIT-X taxi-payment optional scenario.

**PUBEAA** — Public-sector EAA. EAA issued by a public-sector body; named as a possible variant for student attestations (UC 6, UC 11).

**PVID** — Remote Proof of Identity and Liveness Verification. The third-party-mediated identity-proofing solution that UC 9 replaces with EUDIW-based verification.

**QEAA** — Qualified Electronic Attestation of Attributes. See Section 3.

**QTSP** — Qualified Trust Service Provider. The category of trust service provider permitted to issue QEAAs.

**RP** — Relying Party. See Section 2.

**[RTC](https://www.rtcquebec.ca/)** — *Réseau de transport de la Capitale* (Quebec City). Canadian transit operator referenced in UC 11.

**[SARP](https://www.icao.int/about-icao/airnavigationcommission/Pages/how-icao-develops-standards.aspx)** — Standards and Recommended Practices. ICAO-issued normative material; the April 2026 ICAO Facilitation Panel paper proposes new SARPs for DTCs.

**SCA** — Strong Customer Authentication. PSD2-mandated authentication regime for in-scope payments.

**[SD-JWT VC](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-ietf-oauth-sd-jwt-vc/)** — Selective Disclosure JSON Web Token Verifiable Credential. See Section 3.

**SEDIT-X** — Seamless Digital Traveller Experience. The composite multi-episode UC 3, led by the University of the Aegean.

**SSCI** — Self-Service Online Check-In. The pre-airport check-in flow (web or app) addressed by Episode 1 of UC 1.

**T4.2 / T4.3** — Tasks of WP4. T4.2 is the stock-taking and specifications task whose output is D4.1; T4.3 is the implementation task that produces D4.2.

**[TIL](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2980/oj)** — Trusted Issuer List. The cryptographic list of issuers a relying party trusts; established by Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2980. APTITUDE operates a federated TIL across national TILs.

**[TRIP](https://www.icao.int/Security/FAL/TRIP/Pages/default.aspx)** — Traveller Identification Programme (ICAO). The ICAO programme under which Doc 9303, DTC guidance, and TRIP publications fall.

**UC** — Use Case. The atomic unit of pilot specification in WP4; D4.1 specifies 11 UCs.

**[UK ETA](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/apply-for-an-electronic-travel-authorisation-eta)** — United Kingdom Electronic Travel Authorisation. Concrete non-EU instance of the ETA concept consumed by UC 2.

**[WCO](https://www.wcoomd.org/)** — World Customs Organization. Co-author with IATA and ICAO of the API guidelines.

**WSCD** — Wallet Secure Cryptographic Device. The cryptographic component used by the wallet to protect keys; "Local WSCD" denotes the on-device variant adopted by the PagoPA EUDIW.

## Core EUDIW Concepts and Roles

**Actor (in EUDIW terms)** — A party that takes part in a use case in a defined role (Wallet Holder, Wallet Provider, PID Provider, Credential Issuer, Relying Party, Intermediary, Trust Service Provider).

**[Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF)](https://eu-digital-identity-wallet.github.io/eudi-doc-architecture-and-reference-framework/2.8.0/architecture-and-reference-framework-main/)** — The architectural reference document for the EUDIW ecosystem, maintained by the eIDAS Expert Group under the responsibility of the European Commission. Defines the ecosystem components, their interactions, the credential data models, and the protocol profiles. WP4 uses ARF v2.8.0 as its baseline.

**Attestation Provider** — The technical and operational ARF role that issues an attestation (PID, EAA, or QEAA) into a wallet through a standard issuance flow. May be the same legal entity as the Issuer or a delegate of it.

**Attribute** — A single data element carried in a PID or EAA (e.g. *family_name*, *birth_date*, *nationality*, *expiry_date*).

**Carrier Held Biometric** — In UC 9, a biometric reference held within an airport / carrier infrastructure rather than only on the traveller's device; bound to the wallet-presented identity at enrolment.

**Credential Issuer** — The actor that issues a credential (PID, EAA, or QEAA) to the wallet. Can be a Member State authority, a QTSP, an airline, a hotel, a university, etc.

**[Cross-border recognition](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1183/oj)** — eIDAS 2.0 obligation that a wallet issued in one Member State be accepted in any other Member State at the highest level of assurance for in-scope services.

**[Data minimisation](https://gdpr-info.eu/art-5-gdpr/)** — Legally mandated principle (eIDAS 2.0 and GDPR) requiring relying parties to request only the attributes strictly necessary for the service provided.

**EUDIW Intermediary Service** — In SEDIT-X (and similar UC architectures), an intermediary actor that exposes APIs to domain systems, translates between wallet protocols and domain-specific operational APIs, and centralises issuance, verification, and trust checks.

**[European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDIW / EUDI Wallet)](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/eudi-wallet)** — The wallet introduced by Regulation (EU) 2024/1183 that each Member State must provide. Holds the PID and EAAs / QEAAs, supports issuance and presentation flows, and exposes selective disclosure and consent UI to the holder.

**Holder** — The natural or legal person who controls the wallet and to whom credentials are issued. In WP4, almost always a natural person (the traveller, guest, or student).

**Intermediary (ARF)** — A specialised ARF actor operating on behalf of relying parties or issuer-side business systems to handle the complexity of wallet interactions.

**Issuer** — The legal role responsible for issuing a credential or attestation. May be distinct from the technical Attestation Provider.

**[Issuance](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-credential-issuance-1_0.html)** — The flow by which an Attestation Provider delivers a credential into the wallet, typically using OpenID4VCI.

**Member State** — An EU Member State. Each MS is required to provide at least one EUDIW.

**[PID Provider](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2977/oj)** — The infrastructure operated by a national PID issuing authority (or its delegate) that issues the PID into the wallet at the highest level of assurance.

**[Pre-authorised code flow](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-credential-issuance-1_0.html#name-pre-authorized-code-flow)** — OpenID4VCI issuance variant in which the user has been pre-authenticated with the issuer (e.g. via the booking platform) and exchanges a pre-authorised code for the credential. Used in UC 10.

**[Presentation](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-presentations-1_0.html)** — The flow by which the wallet returns a Verifiable Presentation to a Relying Party, typically using OpenID4VP.

**Privacy by design** — eIDAS 2.0 requirement that the wallet protect personal data by default. Implemented through selective disclosure, unobservability, and minimisation of data exposure to wallet providers.

**Proximity flow / Proximity verification** — A presentation in which the wallet and the relying-party reader communicate over a short-range channel (NFC, BLE, QR scan) at the same physical location.

**Relying Party (RP) / Verifier** — The actor that receives and verifies a Verifiable Presentation from a wallet in order to make an operational decision. Must register as such under eIDAS 2.0.

**Remote flow** — A presentation conducted over the internet / web rather than over a proximity channel; typically uses SD-JWT VC and HTTPS.

**Same-device / Cross-device flow** — OpenID4VP engagement modes. Same-device: wallet and relying-party UI on the same device. Cross-device: relying party shows a QR code on a separate screen which the wallet scans.

**Selective disclosure** — Property whereby the wallet holder discloses only the attributes strictly required for a transaction, rather than the full credential. Mandated by eIDAS 2.0 and natively supported by SD-JWT VC and mdoc.

**Trust Framework** — The set of policies, roles, lists, and governance arrangements that allows a relying party to determine whether a presented credential is trustworthy.

**Trust Service Provider** — Entity that provides electronic trust services (signatures, seals, time-stamping, attestations) under eIDAS. Qualified Trust Service Providers (QTSPs) issue QEAAs.

**[Trusted Issuer List (TIL)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2980/oj)** — Cryptographic list of issuers a relying party trusts. Federated across Member States and consumed by RPs at verification time.

**Unobservability / Unlinkability** — Property whereby the wallet provider cannot track which relying parties the holder interacts with. Mandated by eIDAS 2.0.

**User consent** — Explicit, per-presentation approval given by the holder before any attribute is disclosed. Required by eIDAS 2.0 and reinforced under GDPR.

**[Verifiable Credential](https://www.w3.org/TR/vc-data-model-2.0/)** — A cryptographically-signed credential held by the holder; W3C term used together with the format-specific terms SD-JWT VC and mdoc.

**[Verifiable Presentation](https://www.w3.org/TR/vc-data-model-2.0/#presentations)** — The cryptographically-bound output a wallet returns to a relying party, containing the (selectively disclosed) attributes from one or more credentials.

**Wallet Instance / Wallet Unit** — The piece of software running on the holder's device that holds, manages, and presents credentials.

**Wallet Provider** — The Member-State-recognised entity responsible for offering an EUDIW instance. WP4 consumes wallets provided by Member States rather than producing its own.

**Wallet Provider Backend** — The infrastructure operated by the Wallet Provider supporting wallet activation, secure storage, and interfaces with PID and Attestation Providers.

## Credential and Credential-Format Terms

**Biometric Profile attestation** — Wallet attestation issued by the carrier-held biometrics provider in UC 9. Binds the traveller's verified attributes to a biometric reference for use across airport touchpoints.

**Biometric QR Code Credential / Airport Journey Token** — Compact airport-issued token used in the BioQRCode path of SEDIT-X Episode 1. Issued after prior verification of PID and boarding pass; consumed by airport optical-scan infrastructure.

**BioQRCode path** — One of the two SEDIT-X Episode 1 presentation models. The traveller first receives a Biometric QR Code Credential, then uses it as the operational token at airport checkpoints.

**Boarding Pass Credential** — Wallet-stored attestation of the traveller's entitlement to a specific flight. Bound to the holder's PID. Used at check-in, baggage drop, security, and gate.

**Booking Confirmation EAA** — IDnow-issued EAA in UC 10 carrying the booked-stay context. Verified at hotel check-in.

**Booking Reference Credential** — Wallet attestation issued after a hotel reservation is created. Identifies the reservation and supports retrieval at on-site check-in. Used in SEDIT-X Episode 4 and UC 8 / UC 10 variants.

**Campus access credential** — Wallet-held credential granting access to campus buildings or facilities. Verified at turnstiles or door readers.

**[DG1 attributes](https://www.icao.int/publications/pages/publication.aspx?docnum=9303)** — Biographic data drawn from the MRZ of an MRTD (document number, issuing state, document type, expiry date, surname, given names, nationality, date of birth, sex). Used for API transmission.

**[DG2 attributes](https://www.icao.int/publications/pages/publication.aspx?docnum=9303)** — Biometric reference data (typically the facial image) defined by ICAO. Used for biometric verification and biometric-enabled boarding.

**Digital Room Key EAA** — Short-lived EAA in UC 10 carrying the lock_mac_key and an exp claim aligned with the booked stay. Verified by the door reader, possibly fully offline.

**[Digital Travel Credential (DTC)](https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/TRIP/Publications/High-Level-Guidance-explaining-ICAO-DTC.pdf)** — A passport-derived credential defined under ICAO Doc 9303 and integrated into the EUDIW ecosystem through WP3. Carries a subset of MRTD data (document number, MRZ data, biometric reference). Consumed by airline, airport, and border-authority verifiers. Two ICAO types are referenced: DTC Type 1 and DTC Type 2.

**Disability Card / European Disability Card** — EAA attesting to a disability status and any associated entitlement (e.g. a free companion ticket). Used in UC 7 (and UC 8 by extension) with the Italian variant issued by IPZS.

**[Electronic Attestation of Attributes (EAA)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1183/oj)** — eIDAS 2.0 vehicle by which sector-specific attributes (boarding passes, hotel bookings, student status, event tickets) are issued into the wallet and presented to relying parties. Non-qualified by default unless issued by a QTSP under qualified conditions.

**ERASMUS credential** — Student-mobility credential issued by the home university or the ERASMUS IT infrastructure, presented to a host university.

**ERUA Alliance Card** — European Reform University Alliance student credential, issued by member universities of ERUA. Referenced in SEDIT-X Episode 5 and UC 6.

**[European Student Card](https://erasmus-plus.ec.europa.eu/european-student-card-initiative/card)** — Pan-European student credential under the European Student Card Initiative; referenced as one of the credential types for UC 6.

**Facial image / Portrait** — Biometric reference attribute carried in DG2 of a DTC and, in some configurations, in the PID. Used for 1:1 identity matching at boarding gates and biometric checkpoints.

**Ferry Boarding Credential** — Wallet attestation issued by the ferry operator (or participating travel agency) at booking time. Used at the port to validate boarding entitlement, including for vehicle-bearing travellers in conjunction with the mVRC.

**Guest Summary EAA** — IDnow-issued EAA in UC 10 carrying the consolidated guest-registration dataset; presented to the competent authority to discharge the legal guest-registration duty.

**[JPEG 2000](https://jpeg.org/jpeg2000/)** — Image-encoding standard used for the facial image attribute (e.g. in UC 9 attribute set).

**[Machine Readable Travel Document (MRTD)](https://www.icao.int/publications/pages/publication.aspx?docnum=9303)** — Travel document standardised by ICAO Doc 9303 (paper, eMRTD, or DTC).

**[mdoc (ISO/IEC 18013-5)](https://www.iso.org/standard/69084.html)** — A CBOR-encoded, binary-friendly credential format originally designed for the mobile driving licence and now widely adopted for proximity flows. Used at airport gates, hotel kiosks, campus turnstiles, stadium entries, ferry lanes.

**mobile Vehicle Registration Credential (mVRC)** — Wallet attestation about a vehicle and its registration, used in SEDIT-X Episode 3 for ferry-with-vehicle scenarios.

**[Person Identification Data (PID)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2977/oj)** — The foundational, high-assurance identity credential, mandated by Article 5b of eIDAS 2.0 and specified by Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2977. Issued by or on behalf of a national authority. Carries core identity attributes (family name, given name, date of birth, place of birth, nationality, MS-specific identifier). May include a portrait image.

**[PhotoID](https://www.iso.org/standard/74910.html)** — Mobile eID-style identity attestation defined under ISO/IEC 23220, considered alongside the PID and DTC for issuance of the Biometric Profile in UC 9.

**Proximity verification path** — The SEDIT-X Episode 1 variant in which the wallet directly presents PID and Boarding Pass Credential to airport infrastructure via NFC or another proximity channel, without the intermediate Biometric QR Code Credential.

**[Qualified Electronic Attestation of Attributes (QEAA)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1183/oj)** — A qualified variant of the EAA, issued by a QTSP under stricter rules and carrying legal effect equivalent to a paper attestation under eIDAS. Used where a public authority requires qualified status or where cross-border legal effect is required.

**Ready to Travel attestation** — Attestation issued in UC 9 indicating that the traveller has completed all pre-travel verification steps; consumed at airport touchpoints.

**Room Access / Stay-related credential** — SEDIT-X Episode 4 follow-on credentials issued after successful identity verification and check-in, supporting room access and check-out / expense flows.

**[SD-JWT VC](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/draft-ietf-oauth-sd-jwt-vc/)** — Selective Disclosure JSON Web Token Verifiable Credential. JSON-based credential format that supports selective disclosure natively through salted-hash commitments. Used in WP4 for web-mediated and mobile-app flows (online hotel booking, remote ETA application, student-fare eligibility).

**Stadium event ticket** — EAA used in UC 5 representing entry rights to a venue at a specific time.

**Student Status / Student Attestation** — Wallet-held credential (EAA, QEAA, or PUBEAA depending on issuer) attesting to the holder's enrolment as a student at a recognised institution. May include enrolment status, institution, and validity period.

**[W3C Verifiable Credentials Data Model](https://www.w3.org/TR/vc-data-model-2.0/)** — Credential data model defined by W3C; one of the credential model families OpenID4VP supports, alongside ISO mdoc and SD-JWT VC.

## Standards, Protocols, and Technical Specifications

**[Digital Credentials API (DC API)](https://www.w3.org/TR/digital-credentials/)** — A W3C Web Platform API that allows browsers to mediate the issuance and presentation of digital credentials directly from web applications. Extends the Credential Management API and is protocol-agnostic; provides the browser surface through which OpenID4VCI and OpenID4VP can be invoked.

**[ICAO Annex 9 (Facilitation)](https://www.icao.int/Security/FAL/Pages/default.aspx)** — The ICAO Annex covering passenger facilitation and travel-document processes; international context for API/APIS regimes and the basis for the proposed integration of DTCs in April 2026.

**[ICAO Doc 9303 (Machine Readable Travel Documents)](https://www.icao.int/publications/pages/publication.aspx?docnum=9303)** — ICAO standard for MRTDs; defines DG1, DG2, MRZ, and other travel-document data structures.

**[ICAO DTC Guidance](https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/TRIP/Publications/High-Level-Guidance-explaining-ICAO-DTC.pdf)** — High-level ICAO guidance on Digital Travel Credentials; defines DTC Types and the relationship between DTCs and traditional MRTDs.

**[ICAO Facilitation Panel Working Paper on DTCs (April 2026)](https://www.icao.int/Security/FAL/TRIP/Pages/default.aspx)** — Working paper proposing new and revised SARPs for ICAO DTCs to be incorporated into Annex 9.

**[ISO/IEC 18013-5:2021](https://www.iso.org/standard/69084.html)** — Standard defining the mobile driving licence (mDL) application and the in-person (proximity) presentation model for mobile documents (mdocs).

**[ISO/IEC 18013-7](https://www.iso.org/standard/82772.html)** — Add-on to ISO/IEC 18013-5 defining how an mDL can be presented over the internet (remote verification). The 2024 TS has been superseded by a newer version.

**[ISO/IEC 23220](https://www.iso.org/standard/74910.html)** — Series defining mobile eID architecture and data models. Part 1 (2023) provides system architecture; Part 2 (TS, 2026) standardises data objects and encoding rules. Used by PhotoID.

**[OpenID for Verifiable Credential Issuance (OpenID4VCI)](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-credential-issuance-1_0.html)** — OpenID Foundation protocol used between an Attestation Provider and a Wallet Instance to issue credentials. Built on OAuth 2.0. WP4 uses the v1.0-style profile, including the pre-authorised code flow in UC 10.

**[OpenID for Verifiable Presentations (OpenID4VP)](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-presentations-1_0.html)** — OpenID Foundation protocol used between a Wallet Instance and a Relying Party for credential presentation. Built on OAuth 2.0. Supports W3C VCs, ISO mdocs / mDL, and SD-JWT VCs. Designed to work with the W3C Digital Credentials API for browser-mediated flows.

**[WCO–IATA–ICAO API Guidelines](https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/TRIP/Publications/WCO-IATA-ICAO-API-Guidelines.pdf)** — Joint guidelines defining the data elements transmitted under the API regime at the carrier-to-authority interface.

## Legal and Regulatory Instruments

**[Carrier Sanctions Directive — Directive 2001/51/EC](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2001/51/oj)** — Supplements the Schengen Convention and provides the core legal basis for systematic identity checks at boarding (carrier financial liability for transporting passengers without valid travel documents).

**[Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2015/1998](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2015/1998/oj)** — Lays down detailed measures implementing EU aviation security standards under Regulation (EC) No 300/2008.

**[Data Act — Regulation (EU) 2023/2854](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2023/2854/oj)** — Establishes harmonised rules on fair access to and use of data, clarifying who may access which data and under what conditions data may be shared.

**[Directive (EU) 2015/2366 — PSD2](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2015/2366/oj)** — See *PSD2*.

**[Directive (EU) 2022/2555 — NIS2](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2022/2555/oj)** — Sets cybersecurity risk-management and incident-reporting obligations on essential and important entities, including transport, airport, and digital-infrastructure operators.

**[EDPB Opinion 11/2024](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/opinion-board-art-64/opinion-112024-use-facial-recognition-streamline_en)** — European Data Protection Board opinion on the use of facial recognition to streamline airport passenger flows. Assesses compatibility with GDPR Articles 5(1)(e), 5(1)(f), 25, and 32.

**[eIDAS 2.0 — Regulation (EU) 2024/1183](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1183/oj)** — Major revision of the eIDAS framework introducing the EUDIW, an expanded set of trust services, EAAs, electronic ledgers, and archiving services.

**Entente France–Québec** — France–Quebec cooperation arrangement on tuition; the bilateral context of UC 11.

**[ETIAS Regulation — Regulation (EU) 2018/1240](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2018/1240/oj)** — Establishes the EU pre-travel screening system for visa-exempt third-country nationals.

**[Erasmus+ Regulation — Regulation (EU) 2021/817](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2021/817/oj)** — Main governance instrument of the EU's student-mobility programme.

**[EU Artificial Intelligence Act — Regulation (EU) 2024/1689](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj)** — Introduces harmonised rules for AI systems, with specific constraints on biometric identification, risk classification, and safeguards.

**[GDPR — Regulation (EU) 2016/679](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj)** — General Data Protection Regulation. Provides the overarching data-protection framework: data minimisation, purpose limitation, lawful processing, special-category rules for biometrics (Art. 9), DPIA obligation (Art. 35), data protection by design and security of processing (Arts. 25 and 32).

**[Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2977](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2977/oj)** — PID and rule book for the EUDIW. Specifies the PID data model, the issuance process, and the rule book PID providers and wallet providers must follow.

**[Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2979](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2979/oj)** — Integrity and core functionalities of the EUDIW. Specifies minimum security and functional requirements every wallet instance must meet.

**[Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2980](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2980/oj)** — Registration and publication of trusted lists. Specifies the machinery by which PID providers, attestation providers, and relying parties are registered and by which trusted lists are published.

**[Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2981](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2981/oj)** — Certification of the EUDIW.

**[Implementing Regulation (EU) 2024/2982](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2024/2982/oj)** — Protocols and interfaces for the EUDIW. Specifies OpenID4VCI for issuance, OpenID4VP for presentation, and the interfaces wallets, attestation providers, and relying parties must implement.

**[Italian Wallet Technical Specifications](https://italia.github.io/eid-wallet-it-docs/versione-corrente/en/introduction.html)** — Italian national reference specifications for the digital wallet, derived from eIDAS 2.0.

**[Regulation (EC) No 261/2004](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2004/261/oj)** — Establishes air-passenger rights related to compensation and assistance.

**[Regulation (EC) No 300/2008](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2008/300/oj)** — Sets the overarching EU security framework applicable to airports and airlines.

**[Regulation (EU) 2016/399 — Schengen Borders Code](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/399/oj)** — Rules for crossing the Union's external borders; frames the external-border dimension of UC 1 and UC 2.

**[Regulation (EU) 2017/2226 — Entry/Exit System (EES)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2017/2226/oj)** — Establishes the centralised EU system for recording entries and exits of third-country nationals at Schengen external borders.

**[Regulation (EU) 2024/1183](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1183/oj)** — See *eIDAS 2.0*.

**[Regulation (EU) 2025/12](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2025/12/oj)** — Updates the carrier-to-border-authority API regime, replacing Directive 2004/82/EC.

**[Regulation (EU) 2025/13](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2025/13/oj)** — APIS for terrorism / serious crime; complements Regulation (EU) 2025/12 with law-enforcement use cases (amends Regulation (EU) 2019/818).

**[Regulation (EU) No 1177/2010](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2010/1177/oj)** — Defines passenger rights for sea and inland-waterway transport.

**[Resolución de 22 de julio de 2021 (BOE-A-2021-14195)](https://www.boe.es/buscar/doc.php?id=BOE-A-2021-14195)** — Spanish National Civil Aviation Security Programme; national operationalisation of Regulation (EC) No 300/2008 and Implementing Regulation (EU) 2015/1998.

**[UK Electronic Travel Authorisation](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/apply-for-an-electronic-travel-authorisation-eta)** — UK administrative travel-screening system; concrete non-EU instance of the ETA concept consumed by UC 2.
