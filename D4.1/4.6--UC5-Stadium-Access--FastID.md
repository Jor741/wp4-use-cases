## UC 5: Stadium Access (FastID)

The FastID use case sits within the Student Access scenario and looks at how a DTC, derived from a verified identity in the EUDI Wallet, can be used together with an access credential to support access to controlled stadium areas for both fans and employees. The focus in WP4 is on making stadium access flows work in a way where identity verification, credential issuance and physical entry fit together without adding unnecessary friction. The implementation is based on a live stadium environment where FastID is already used in production for identity verification and access.

The flow starts with the user creating an N.E.C. account and getting access to the FastID wallet through that process. The user then onboards their identity in the wallet through a check on their identity document, which results in a PID-based identity. From there, a DTC is generated and linked to the user’s identity attributes. After that, a football ticket credential is issued into the wallet, representing the permission to access specific stadium areas and being tied to both the identity and the DTC. In practice, both credentials are used together when access is requested.

At the access point, the user presents the credential via the wallet, typically through a QR-based flow. The system checks validity and if everything matches the expected identity context, which then grants access. The setup also supports biometric verification flows, where the enrolled identity can be matched during access. The same approach applies to both fans and employees, including interns and temporary staff, which is why the scenario is also relevant for student-related roles and potential student discount or access entitlements.

From a scenario point of view, UC 5 shows how wallet identity, DTC-based credentials and access rights can be combined to streamline stadium access in a way that stays interoperable, works in a live operational environment, and can also support student-related access and entitlement use cases without changing the core access flow.
