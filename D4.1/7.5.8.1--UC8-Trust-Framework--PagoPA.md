#### Trust Framework

UC8 implement a trust framework that is strategically aligned with the **eIDAS 2.0 and the EUDI Wallet trust model**, ensuring that **Trenitalia**, acting as the Relying Party, can verify credentials with the highest level of assurance. While the target ecosystem relies on the EUDIW infrastructure, this use case refers to the APTITUDE Trust Framework. This allows Trenitalia to recognize IPZS, Istituto Poligrafico e Zecca dello Stato, as a **trusted issuer** and to validate the authenticity of the PID, DTC.

In practice, the trust framework within UC8 is an integrated component of the verification flow, governed by the following pillars:
- **Issuer Recognition**: Trenitalia validates that the credentials (PID, DTC) originate from the recognized Italian Public Issuer via the APTITUDE pilot Trust List.
- **Cryptographic & Status Validation**: the system performs real-time checks to ensure the credential signature is valid and that the attestation has not been revoked, suspended, or expired, leveraging the Status List managed by the issuer.

By adopting the OpenID4VP standard for bot remote and proximity presentations, UC8 demonstrates a realistic interoperability pattern, ensuring that the trust established during the pilot remains conceptually consistent with the future production EUDI Wallet ecosystem, where trust is derived from a common European framework rather than isolated bilateral agreements.

This framework allows UC8 to demonstrate how personal identification and eligibility status can be verified securely, ensuring that identity related services are only granted upon a trust validation.
