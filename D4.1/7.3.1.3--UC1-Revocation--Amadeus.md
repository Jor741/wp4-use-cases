#### Revocation
Amadeus **does not issue, manage, suspend, or revoke** any digital credentials.  
The use case relies exclusively on **PID** and **DTC** credentials that are:
- Issued by **trusted national authorities**
- Stored and presented by the traveler through the **EUDI Wallet (EUDIW)**

As a relying party, Amadeus **consumes verification results** but does not operate any revocation services or credential lifecycle management functions.


When a **PID or DTC** is revoked by its issuing authority, the revocation is reflected at the **trust and validation layer**, not inside Amadeus systems.



If a PID or DTC used in the *Board the Flight* use case is **revoked, expired, or otherwise invalid**, this is detected as part of the **standard verification process**, both online and on‑site.

#### Examples of Revocation

Digital credentials used in the *Amadeus Board the Flight* use case (PID or DTC) may be revoked by their **original issuer** for legal, security, or administrative reasons. Typical examples include:

##### 1. Loss or Theft of the Physical Travel Document
- The traveler reports a **lost or stolen passport or ID card**.
- The issuing authority revokes the associated **PID and/or DTC**.
- Any subsequent wallet presentation fails verification.

##### 2. Expiration of the Travel Document
- The underlying passport or ID card **expires**.
- The corresponding **DTC becomes invalid** due to expiration.
- Verification fails based on validity checks, without explicit revocation handling by Amadeus.

##### 3. Passport or ID Renewal
- The traveler renews or replaces their passport or national ID.
- The previous document number is invalidated.
- The issuer revokes the old PID/DTC and issues a **new credential**.

##### 4. Identity Fraud or Compromise
- The issuing authority detects **fraudulent use**, cloning, or compromise of identity data.
- The affected PID or DTC is revoked immediately.
- Any attempt to use the revoked credential is rejected during verification.

##### 5. Administrative or Legal Invalidation
- A legal or administrative decision requires **invalidating an identity record** (e.g. incorrect data).
- The issuer revokes the existing PID/DTC pending correction or re‑issuance.

##### 6. Wallet or Device Security Compromise
- A credential is determined to be linked to a **compromised device or wallet instance**.
- The issuer revokes the credential and requires re‑enrollment on a trusted device.


### Episode 1 – Online Verification at SSCI

During **Self‑Service Online Check‑In**:

- The verifier checks:
  - Credential validity period
  - Issuer trust
  - Credential status or revocation indicators
- If the credential is revoked or invalid:
  - The verifiable presentation **fails validation**
  - The relying party **rejects the credential**
  - Online check‑in **cannot proceed using the wallet flow**
  - The traveler is redirected to **alternative or manual handling**



### Episode 2 – Proximity Verification at the Airport

During **boarding at the airport**:

- The verifier performs:
  - Cryptographic validation
  - Trust anchor and issuer checks
  - Validity and status verification
- If the credential is revoked or invalid:
  - The proximity verification **fails**
  - The credential is **not accepted** for boarding
  - The traveler is redirected to **staff‑assisted procedures**
