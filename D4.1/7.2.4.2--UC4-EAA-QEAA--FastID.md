#### Electronic Attestations of Attributes (EAA / QEAA)

The main credential used in this use case is the access credential, issued as a non-qualified Electronic Attestation of Attributes (EAA).

This credential is issued by FastID, acting as the airport issuer in the pilot setup. It represents the access rights of the holder to specific airport locations and is linked to the verified identity through the DTC.

The DTC (Digital Travel Credential) is used as an intermediate step in the flow, to allow selective disclosure. It is created from the PID and used as the basis for issuing the access credential. This allows the system to rely on a verified identity without repeating the full identity verification at each access point.

The intended verifier is the airport access system (the Schiphol Lab environment), which requests the credential from the wallet and validates it before granting access.

The following attributes will define the EAA
- family_name
- access_rights 
- validity_period
- portrait
