### UC 1: Board the flight (Amadeus)



The *Board the Flight* use case is grounded in a combination of **EU digital identity regulation**, **data protection and cybersecurity frameworks**, and **aviation security and facilitation standards**. The key reference documents are outlined below.

---

#### European Digital Identity and Trust Framework

- **eIDAS Regulation (incl. eIDAS 2.0)**  
  The eIDAS framework provides the **legal basis** for the European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDIW), including:
  - Person Identification Data (PID)
  - Electronic Attestations of Attributes (EAA)
  - Cross‑border recognition of wallet‑based identification and trust services

- **The Major Revision “eIDAS 2.0 / EUDIW” – Regulation (EU) 2024/1183**  
  This regulation fundamentally updates eIDAS by introducing:
  - The European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDIW)
  - An expanded set of trust services
  - Electronic attestations of attributes, electronic ledgers, and archiving services

- **eIDAS Implementing Acts (updates)**  
  Implementing Acts specify binding technical and procedural requirements, including:
  - Standards and technical specifications
  - Registration of wallet‑relying parties
  - Rules for issuing, presenting, and verifying electronic attestations of attributes

- **EUDI Wallet Architecture and Reference Framework (ARF)**  
  The ARF serves as the technical toolbox defining:
  - Wallet architecture and roles
  - Trust models and governance
  - Privacy‑by‑design principles (selective disclosure, unlinkability)
  - Interoperability requirements across Member States

---


#### Technical Standards



- **OpenID for Verifiable Presentations (OpenID4VP)**

    **OpenID4VP** is a protocol from the OpenID Foundation that enables a **verifier** (e.g. a website or service) to securely request and receive **verifiable presentations** from a **holder’s wallet**. It is built on **OAuth 2.0** and supports multiple credential formats, including **W3C Verifiable Credentials**, **ISO mdocs / mDL (ISO/IEC 18013‑5)**, and **SD‑JWT VCs**.  
    Its core value is **privacy‑preserving, user‑controlled presentation** with **selective disclosure**, supporting both **same‑device** and **cross‑device (QR‑based)** flows.  
    OpenID4VP is also designed to work **in conjunction with the W3C Digital Credentials API** for browser‑mediated flows. [1](https://openid.net/specs/openid-4-verifiable-presentations-1_0.html)



- **W3C Digital Credentials API (DC API)**

    The **Digital Credentials API (DC API)** is a **W3C Web Platform API** that allows **browsers (user agents)** to mediate the **issuance and presentation of digital credentials** directly from web applications.  
    It extends the **Credential Management API** and is **protocol‑agnostic**, acting as a bridge between websites and user wallets, while enforcing **explicit user consent**, **data minimisation**, and **secure UX‑mediated flows**.  

    The DC API does **not replace protocols** like OpenID4VP; instead, it provides the **browser surface** through which protocols such as **OpenID4VP and OpenID4VCI** can be invoked in a consistent and secure way on the web. [2](https://www.w3.org/TR/digital-credentials/)

- **ISO/IEC 18013 series**  
  Standards related to mobile documents and proximity/online verification concepts used as reference for identity and travel credentials.

  ISO/IEC 18013‑5:2021 defines the mobile driving licence (mDL) application and the in‑person (proximity) presentation model for mobile documents (mdocs). It specifies how a mobile device presents an identity document securely to a nearby reader.

  ISO/IEC 18013‑7 augments 18013‑5 by defining how an mDL can be presented over the internet (remote verification). It is a formal add‑on to the proximity model. The 2024 TS was subsequently revised; a newer version supersedes it

- **ISO/IEC 23220**  
  
    ISO/IEC 23220 defines the mobile eID architecture and data models used by **PhotoID**, with ISO/IEC 23220‑1:2023 providing system architecture and ISO/IEC TS 23220‑2:2026 standardizing data objects and encoding rules

#### Data Protection and Fundamental Rights

- **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) – Regulation (EU) 2016/679**  
  Provides the overarching data protection framework, including:
  - Data minimisation
  - Purpose limitation
  - Lawful processing and consent requirements

- **GDPR Article 9 – Biometric Data**  
  Facial images and biometric templates are classified as **special category personal data**, requiring enhanced safeguards and, where applicable, Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA).

- **EDPB Opinion 11/2024** on the use of facial recognition to streamline airport passenger flows  
  Assesses the compatibility of biometric processing with:
  - Articles 5(1)(e) and 5(1)(f) GDPR
  - Articles 25 and 32 GDPR (data protection by design and security of processing)

- **Data Act – Regulation (EU) 2023/2854**  
  Establishes harmonised rules on fair access to and use of data, clarifying:
  - Who may access which data
  - Under what conditions data may be shared across stakeholders

---

#### Aviation, Border, and Passenger‑Related Regulation

- **Regulation (EU) 2025/12** on Advance Passenger Information (border checks)  
  Replaces Directive 2004/82/EC and governs the collection and transmission of API data for border control purposes.

- **Regulation (EU) 2025/13** on Advance Passenger Information for terrorism and serious crime  
  Complements border‑control API requirements with law‑enforcement use cases.

- **Regulation (EC) No 300/2008** on common rules in the field of civil aviation security  
  Sets the overarching security framework applicable to airports and airlines.

- **Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2015/1998**  
  Lays down detailed measures for implementing EU aviation security standards.

- **Regulation (EC) No 261/2004**  
  Establishes air passenger rights related to compensation and assistance.

- **Regulation (EU) No 1177/2010**  
  Defines passenger rights for sea and inland waterway transport (referenced for cross‑modal consistency).

---

#### Cybersecurity and Emerging Technology Regulation

- **NIS2 Directive – Directive (EU) 2022/2555**  
  Sets cybersecurity risk‑management and incident‑reporting obligations for essential and important entities, including actors in digital and transport infrastructure.

- **EU Artificial Intelligence Act – Regulation (EU) 2024/1689**  
  Introduces harmonised rules for AI systems, including **specific constraints on biometric systems**, particularly regarding:
  - Remote biometric identification
  - Risk classification and safeguards
  - Interaction with GDPR obligations

---

#### Aviation and Travel Standards




- **ICAO Facilitation Panel Working Paper on Digital Travel Credentials (April 2026)**

    An ICAO Facilitation Panel working paper presented in **April 2026** explicitly proposes **new and revised definitions**, as well as **Standards and Recommended Practices (SARPs)** for **ICAO Digital Travel Credentials (DTCs)** to be incorporated into **Annex 9 — Facilitation**.

    The objective of this proposal is to formally include **Digital Travel Credentials** within the ICAO regulatory framework, in a manner comparable to **Machine‑Readable Travel Documents (MRTDs)** and **electronic MRTDs (eMRTDs)**. By doing so, the working paper seeks to ensure that DTCs are governed by ICAO SARPs and achieve a level of **global interoperability, integrity, and security** appropriate for facilitation, border control, and passenger clearance processes. [1](https://www.icao.int/sites/default/files/Meetings/FALP14/Working%20Papers/WP%2021%20-%20Revised%20definitions%20and%20proposed%20standards%20and%20recommended%20practices%20for%20ICAO%20Digital%20Travel%20Credentials/FALP14-WP-21.E.pdf)

- **IATA Specifications and Guidance**
  - IATA One ID initiative
  - Industry guidance on identity management, passenger facilitation, and interoperability  
  These materials are consolidated and monitored through an internal standards and regulations tracking document used within the project.

---



These reference documents collectively establish the **legal, technical, and operational foundation** for the *Board the Flight* use case, ensuring alignment with EU digital identity policy, aviation requirements, data protection obligations, and international travel standards.
