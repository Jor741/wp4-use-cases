## 10.4 Roadmap Towards D4.3 (Validation Report)

This section describes the planned activities and the timeline that will carry WP4 from the implementation manual due as D4.2 (Section 10.3) to the validation report due as **D4.3 — UC Validation Report** at **Milestone 16 (M22)**.

### Purpose of the roadmap

D4.1 specifies *what* WP4 will pilot. D4.2 specifies *how* the pilot will be built. D4.3 will report on *whether the pilot worked*. The roadmap presented here identifies the activities of Task T4.4 (Piloting, User Testing, and UC Evaluation), the partners responsible for each activity, the testing approach, the KPI framework against which each UC will be evaluated, and the feedback-collection mechanisms that will support the assessment.

### Task T4.4 — Piloting, User Testing, and UC Evaluation

Under the Grant Agreement, Task T4.4 is led by GRNET as the WP4 coordinator and runs after T4.3, from the production of D4.2 to the production of D4.3. Its objective is to execute the pilots prepared during T4.3, to document the results, and to evaluate each use case against its stated objectives. T4.4 has the following main strands of work.

* **Pilot execution.** Each UC is exercised end to end with real users (or pilot users acting under realistic conditions) in the participating Member States. The pilot environment, the test data, the user-recruitment procedures, and the consent forms produced under T4.3 are used during this phase.
* **KPI recording.** For every UC, the KPIs defined in the UC specification sheet (Annex A) are recorded throughout the pilot. The KPI framework is described below in dedicated paragraphs.
* **User testing and feedback collection.** Pilot users are invited to provide feedback on the wallet-based variant of the journey, both quantitatively (via structured questionnaires) and qualitatively (via interviews and observation sessions). The feedback is collected on a UC-by-UC basis and consolidated for cross-UC analysis.
* **Cross-border interoperability tests.** The cross-border legs of the relevant UCs (notably UC 6 across IT, FR, EL; UC 7 and UC 8; the Canadian dimension of UC 11; the cross-Member-State variants of UC 1 and UC 4) are exercised explicitly, to test the cross-border recognition obligation of eIDAS 2.0 in concrete settings.
* **Compliance review of the executed pilots.** WP7 conducts a compliance review of the actually executed flows, complementing the design-time review carried out earlier in the project. Findings inform the conclusions of D4.3 and are fed back to the European Commission and to the relevant national authorities.
* **Cross-UC and cross-scenario synthesis.** GRNET, working with all UC leads, synthesises the per-UC findings into a cross-UC analysis: which patterns recur across the pilots, which findings are sector-specific, what the pilot has learned about the EUDIW ecosystem at the level of the three WP4 scenarios.
* **Drafting and review of D4.3.** The validation report is incrementally assembled as the pilot progresses, with each UC contributing its own chapter and GRNET producing the cross-cutting analysis.

### Testing approach

The testing approach in T4.4 builds directly on the integration tests carried out during T4.3 on the WP2 testbed and extends them along three dimensions.

* **From conformance to operation.** T4.3 verifies that each UC implementation is conformant to the technical profiles. T4.4 verifies that the UC works under realistic operational conditions, including network connection variability, peak loads at touchpoints (boarding gate, hotel front desk, campuses), and recovery from common error situations.
* **Happy and unhappy paths.** Each UC specification sheet (Annex A) documents a set of unhappy paths. T4.4 exercises the most significant unhappy paths in addition to the happy path, so that D4.3 can report on resilience and not only on best-case behaviour.
* **End user perspective.** T4.4 introduces the user perspective: pilot users perform the journey themselves, with the support and observation of the UC leads and the partner organisations. The user experience becomes a first-class evaluation dimension, alongside technical correctness.

### KPI framework

D4.3 will report each UC's results against a KPI framework that is shaped during T4.3 and finalised at the start of T4.4. The framework will combine four families of indicators, applied uniformly across all 11 UCs.

* **Functional KPIs.** Whether the UC's main flow completes successfully, what proportion of attempts succeed, and what the failure modes are. Examples: completion rate of the boarding-pass-in-wallet flow at the gate; success rate of the hotel check-in via QR-code; fraction of student-fare purchases completed entirely within the wallet.
* **Performance KPIs.** End-to-end response times at each touchpoint, with a focus on the touchpoints that are time-sensitive in production (airport gates, hotel kiosks, train conductors during boarding, ferry boarding lanes).
* **User-experience KPIs.** Quantitative scores from structured questionnaires (perceived ease of use, perceived speed, perceived trust), and qualitative findings from observation and interviews. Comparison with the legacy process is made wherever feasible.
* **Cross-border KPIs.** Specific indicators tracking whether wallets and PIDs issued in one Member State are accepted at relying parties in another Member State, including time-to-acceptance and any operational adjustments required at the relying-party side.

The exact list of KPIs per UC is included in the UC specification sheets in Annex A and refined during T4.3.

### Feedback collection mechanisms

T4.4 collects feedback from three categories of stakeholders, mirroring the categories engaged during the stock-taking phase (Section 3.2).

* **Pilot users** provide structured questionnaire responses after each UC journey, supplemented by qualitative interviews for a subset of users.
* **Relying-party staff** (front-desk operators, gate agents, train conductors, kiosk operators) provide feedback on the operational impact of the wallet-based variant: training requirements, error-handling, fallback to legacy procedures, and support burden.
* **Credential issuers and integrators** provide feedback on the implementation effort, on the reliability of the WP2/WP3/WP6 interfaces, and on the issues that surfaced only at scale.

The feedback channels (questionnaires, interview templates, issue trackers) are prepared during T4.3 and operated during T4.4.

### Indicative timeline towards M22

The Grant Agreement places D4.3 at **Milestone 16 (M22)**. The intermediate checkpoints below are indicative and will be refined during T4.3 and at the start of T4.4.

| Period | Activity |
|---|---|
| **M18** | Submission of D4.2. Start of T4.4. KPI framework finalised. Pilot environments and feedback channels operational. |
| Early T4.4 | First pilot runs per UC. Initial KPI capture and first user-feedback rounds. Adjustments to the operational procedures based on early findings. |
| Mid T4.4 | Sustained pilot operation, including cross-border legs. Iterative collection of KPI data and user feedback. Drafting of per-UC chapters of D4.3 begins. |
| Pre-M22 | Synthesis across UCs. Cross-cutting findings consolidated. Internal review of D4.3. WP7 compliance review of the executed pilots. |
| **M22** | **Submission of D4.3 (Milestone 16).** |

The timeline assumes that D4.2 is delivered on schedule at M18 and that the pre-conditions identified in Section 10.3 have been met. Slippage in those pre-conditions will reduce the pilot window in T4.4, which is treated as a risk in Section 9.1.

### Hand-offs and responsibilities

The transition from D4.2 to D4.3 involves the same actor categories as the D4.1 → D4.2 transition, with adjusted responsibilities for the piloting and validation phase.

* **UC leads** execute the pilot for their UC, capture the KPIs, collect the feedback, and produce the per-UC validation chapters of D4.3.
* The **WP4 coordinator (GRNET)** owns the cross-cutting validation chapters, the cross-UC synthesis, the recommendations, and the assembly and review of D4.3.
* **Other Work Packages** contribute as in T4.3:
  * **WP2** supports the analysis of conformance issues that surface during the pilot.
  * **WP3** and **WP6** participate in the analysis of the cross-WP touchpoints exercised during the pilot.
  * **WP7** conducts the compliance review of the executed pilots and contributes the corresponding section of D4.3.

### Risks specific to the D4.2 → D4.3 path

Two risks specific to this transition deserve a dedicated mention; they are treated alongside the other WP4 risks in Section 9.1.

* **Insufficient pilot user volume.** A pilot run with too few users does not produce statistically meaningful KPIs. The mitigation is to plan the user-recruitment procedure in T4.3 with explicit volume targets per UC, and to start the recruitment well before M18.
* **Late surfacing of regulatory or operational blockers.** A blocker discovered during the pilot (for example, a national authority refusing to accept a credential format, or a relying party's IT system failing under realistic load) can interrupt the pilot. The mitigation is to flag any such risk early through the open-issues mechanism of Section 10.2 and to keep a fallback procedure for each UC in the pilot plan.

### Outlook beyond D4.3

D4.3 closes the WP4 deliverable cycle but does not close the EUDIW deployment journey. The recommendations and the lessons learned in D4.3 feed into the broader APTITUDE outputs (notably D1.1, the roadmap for the implementation of the ecosystem, and D1.2, the sustainability business strategy) and into the European Commission's preparations for the 2026 wallet-deployment target. The pilot results are also expected to inform follow-on initiatives in the EUDIW ecosystem, including the work of other Large Scale Pilots and of standardisation bodies on the topics surfaced by the WP4 pilots.
