## UC 10 — Streamlined End-to-End Hotel Booking: ARF Alignment

UC 10 implements the ARF baseline selected by WP2 and adheres to the common WP4 protocol profiles. This subsection records the components, profiles, and data-model choices specific to UC 10, and the points where the use case refines or extends the common baseline.

### ARF components exercised

| ARF Component | UC 10 instantiation |
| --- | --- |
| Wallet Instance | Certified EUDIW instance held by the guest. UC 10 is exercised cross-border with at least two Member State wallet instances in scope; UC 10 consumes wallet instances and does not develop them. |
| Wallet Provider Backend | Backend(s) of the participating EUDIW provider(s), consumed as-is. |
| PID Provider | National PID issuer of the guest's Member State; trust resolved via the federation described in Section 7.5. UC 10 requires PID provider availability for both same-state and cross-border guests. |
| (Q)EAA Attestation Provider | IDnow GmbH acts as the single attestation provider for UC 10, issuing three EAAs under a qualified electronic seal: Booking Confirmation EAA, Digital Room Key EAA, and Guest Summary EAA. |
| Relying Party | Four distinct RPs: (i) Booking Platform (online); (ii) Hotel check-in system (proximity, online); (iii) Door reader (proximity, offline-capable); (iv) Competent authority interface for guest-registration reporting. |
| Trust Service Providers / Trusted-List Operators | APTITUDE pilot TIL (for IDnow EAA issuer trust) and the federated national PID TILs (for PID issuer trust), per Section 7.5. The door reader component additionally consumes a locally cached subset of the TIL to enable offline verification — this is the principal UC 10 refinement of the common baseline. |

### Protocol profiles applied

| Profile | UC 10 use |
| --- | --- |
| OpenID4VCI | Used for all three EAA issuance flows. UC 10 exercises (i) the pre-authorised code flow — selected because issuance is always triggered by a trusted RP that has already verified the wallet holder via PID; and (ii) the batch issuance variant in Phase 3, where the Digital Room Key EAA and the Guest Summary EAA are issued in a single transaction at check-in. |
| OpenID4VP | Used for all four presentations: PID at booking (remote), PID + Booking Confirmation at check-in (proximity-initiated via QR), Digital Room Key at the door (proximity), and Guest Summary at authority reporting (remote). |
| ISO/IEC 18013-5 (mdoc) bindings | Used at the door reader (Phase 4) for proximity engagement over NFC/BLE. UC 10 applies the standard 18013-5 transport bindings but carries an SD-JWT VC payload (see data-model notes below). |
| QR engagement (ISO/IEC 18004) | Used at the hotel kiosk to initiate the Phase 3 OID4VP flow. |

### Data model choice (mdoc vs SD-JWT VC)

UC 10 uses **SD-JWT VC** as the credential format for all three IDnow-issued EAAs and for the PID consumed by UC 10 RPs. Rationale:

- The Booking Confirmation, Digital Room Key, and Guest Summary EAAs are designed for selective disclosure (e.g., the Guest Summary discloses only the attribute subset required by the specific national authority interface), which SD-JWT VC supports natively.
- All four UC 10 RPs are backend services capable of JSON/JWT processing; no UC 10 RP is constrained to mdoc-only.
- The door reader (Phase 4) uses the ISO/IEC 18013-5 transport bindings to carry an SD-JWT VC payload, rather than the mdoc serialisation. This is a UC 10–specific combination and is recorded here so that WP2 testbed conformance tests target the correct profile pairing.

This combination is recorded in the credential inventory of Section 7.2.

### UC 10–specific refinements of the common baseline

- **Offline relying-party verification.** The door reader (Phase 4) operates in an offline-capable mode: it pre-caches IDnow's issuer key from the TIL during periodic online sync windows and validates the SD-JWT VC signature locally without contacting the TIL at presentation time. This refines the trust-evaluation pattern described in the common baseline, where TIL resolution is assumed to be online.
- **RP-authentication policy at the door.** Per the IDnow HLR, the wallet shares the Digital Room Key EAA at the door even if RP authentication fails, prioritising guest convenience for the door-unlock interaction. This is a deliberate, documented deviation from the default ARF guidance and is logged in the wallet's transaction log.
- **Batch issuance at check-in.** UC 10 exercises the OpenID4VCI batch-issuance profile to issue two EAAs in a single transaction at check-in, in order to meet the latency requirements of the on-site flow.
- **Short-lived credential lifecycle.** The Digital Room Key EAA carries a short-lived `exp` aligned with the booked stay window; revocation is therefore primarily handled by expiry rather than by an active revocation mechanism, which is a UC 10–specific simplification of the lifecycle pattern.
