#### Trust Framework
In UC4 trust is based on whether airport systems can rely on the credentials presented through the FastID wallet to grant access to secured areas.

The approach follows the eIDAS 2.0 / EUDI Wallet trust principles, where relying parties do not establish individual trust relationships with each issuer, instead they rely on a shared framework that allows them to verify whether a credential can be accepted at the time of use.

In practice the RP validates:
- That the DTC originates from a recognised wallet (Masterlist)
- The Access Credential is issued within the trusted pilot setup
- None of the credentials have expired or been revoked
- That the data presented matches the access context

For the pilot this is implemented through a controlled Trust List. It allows the system to simulate issuer recognition and trust decisions in a way that reflects the expected production model.

Trust checks are part of the normal access flow and are performed at each verification point, either directly by the access system or through a supporting verification service.

Overall, UC4 relies on a simple trust chain (PID → DTC → Access Credential), which is validated consistently across the journey and aligned with the general direction of the EUDI Wallet trust framework.
