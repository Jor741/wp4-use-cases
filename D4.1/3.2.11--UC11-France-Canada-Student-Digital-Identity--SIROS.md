### UC 11: France-Canada Student Digital Identity (SIROS)
Engagement for this use case has, to date, brought together stakeholders on both the Canadian and French sides, covering the credential issuance, ecosystem governance, and secure-identity-document perspectives. Consultations were conducted through bilateral exchanges with the organisations listed below, aimed at scoping the use case and clarifying requirements.
The following stakeholders have been involved so far:

**Digital ID & Authentication Council of Canada (DIACC)** — engagement with the President and a Development Officer. In addition to providing input on the Canadian digital-identity ecosystem, governance arrangements, and interoperability expectations, DIACC is leading outreach on the Canadian side to identify and onboard a suitable relying party.

**GYSC** (global youth and student community in France) — engagement with the Director, offering the issuer perspective on the student card, its lifecycle, and the attributes relevant to cross-border use.

**France Titres — Agence Nationale des Titres Sécurisés (ANTS)** — engagement on the French side, covering the secure-identity-document and state-issuer viewpoint, and the conditions under which the use case could be supported within the French framework.

A Canadian relying party (i.e. a service provider that would consume the credential on the Canadian side) has not yet been onboarded. DIACC is coordinating this outreach, and the analysis in the following chapters is therefore based on the issuer and ecosystem inputs gathered so far, with the relying-party perspective flagged as an open item to be closed before the findings are finalised.
