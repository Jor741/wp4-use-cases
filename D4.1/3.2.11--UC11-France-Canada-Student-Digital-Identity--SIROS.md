### UC 11: France-Canada Student Digital Identity (SIROS)

Engagement for UC 11 has, to date, brought together stakeholders on both the French and Canadian sides, covering credential issuance, ecosystem governance, and the secure-identity-document perspective. Consultations were conducted through bilateral exchanges aimed at scoping the use case, clarifying requirements, and testing feasibility.

- **Digital ID & Authentication Council of Canada (DIACC)** — engagement with the President and a Development Officer. In addition to providing input on the Canadian digital-identity ecosystem, governance arrangements, and interoperability expectations, DIACC is leading the outreach on the Canadian side to identify and onboard a suitable relying party.

- **GYSC** — engagement with the Director, offering the issuer perspective on the French student card (an EAA), its lifecycle, and the attributes relevant to cross-border use.

- **France Titres — Agence Nationale des Titres Sécurisés (ANTS)** — engagement on the French side, covering the secure-identity-document and state-issuer viewpoint, and the conditions under which the use case can be supported within the French framework.

- **Quebec regional government** — named as a UC partner via the existing ANTS–Quebec cooperation arrangement that underpins the pilot. **[TBC: direct consultation specific to UC 11 has not yet been recorded; ANTS is expected to lead that outreach.]**

A Canadian relying party (a service provider that would consume the credential on the Canadian side) has not yet been formally onboarded. DIACC is coordinating this outreach. The analysis below therefore draws on the issuer and ecosystem inputs gathered so far, with the relying-party perspective flagged as an open item to be closed before the findings are finalised.
