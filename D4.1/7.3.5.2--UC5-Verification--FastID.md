#### Verification

UC5 includes both remote and proximity verification.

The remote verification happens before the user arrives at the stadium, which is required to issue the access credential. In this case, the relying party requests the required wallet attributes through an OpenID4VP standard. The holder reviews the request, approves the sharing, and sends the requested data to the relying party. The RP then checks the received data and issues or confirms the access credential by sending a ticket to the app.

The proximity verification flow is used at physical stadium accesspoints, such as entrances or restricted internal areas. The wallet holder presents the credential using QR or NFC. The verifier checks the credential authenticity (masterlist), validity period, and access rights linked to the holder.

In some cases the verification can also include a biometric comparison to confirm that the person presenting the credential is the same person linked to the wallet. The exact level of biometric verification depends on the operational setup used during the live system.
