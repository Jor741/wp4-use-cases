#### Trust Framework

For **UC3: Seamless Digital Traveller Experience (SEDIT-X)**, the trust framework explains how WP4 relying parties determine whether a presented credential can be accepted as trustworthy in a **cross-border** and **multi-domain** travel context.

At the **target ecosystem level**, UC3 is aligned with the **eIDAS 2.0 / EUDI Wallet trust model**. In that model, trust does not depend on bilateral arrangements between each verifier and each issuer, but on a common European trust framework in which the verifier can determine whether:

- the credential was issued by a recognised and trusted issuer,
- the credential signature is valid,
- the credential is still valid at the time of presentation,
- the credential has not been revoked or suspended, and
- the credential is being presented in a context consistent with the applicable wallet and relying-party rules.

For **APTITUDE piloting purposes**, this production trust framework is not yet replaced by the final operational eIDAS 2.0 ecosystem. Instead, the pilot uses an **APTITUDE trust framework** based on a **pilot Trust List (TL)** that mimics the logic of an eIDAS-style trust list. This allows WP4 participants to test trust establishment, issuer recognition, and cross-border verification flows in a controlled but realistic environment.

In practice, WP4 relying parties in UC3 — such as airport systems, ferry operators, hotel check-in systems, and university-side verifiers — determine trust by applying a combination of issuer recognition, cryptographic validation, and status checking before making an operational decision. For piloting purposes, issuer recognition is supported through the APTITUDE pilot Trust List, while the broader trust decision also includes the remaining validation checks required for the presented credential.

The same trust approach supports the cross-border nature of SEDIT-X. A verifier in one Member State or operational domain does not need to pre-configure separate trust relationships for every foreign issuer. Instead, it relies on the shared pilot trust framework to recognise which issuers are trusted for the purposes of the pilot and which credential types they are authorised to issue.

Within UC3, this trust validation is typically performed by the verifier itself or by an **EUDIW Intermediary Service** acting on behalf of the relying party. The trust decision is therefore part of the normal verification flow and is combined with signature validation, issuer-chain validation, and status or revocation checks.

Overall, the UC3 trust framework should therefore be understood as:

- strategically aligned with the eIDAS 2.0 / EUDI Wallet trust model; and
- operationally approximated in the pilot through the APTITUDE trust framework, including the pilot Trust 
- List and the related validation services used during credential verification.

This approach ensures that UC3 can demonstrate realistic trust and interoperability patterns during piloting, while remaining conceptually consistent with the trust framework expected in the future production EUDI Wallet ecosystem.
