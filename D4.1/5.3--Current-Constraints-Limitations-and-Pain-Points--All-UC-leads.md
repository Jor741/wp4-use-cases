## Current Constraints, Limitations and Pain Points (All UC leads)

**UC6**
------------------
Current approaches to identity matching and student status verification are fragmented, inconsistent, and often limited to national or institutional contexts. Students frequently rely on **physical cards or non-standardised digital credentials**, which are not always recognised across borders or by different service providers. This leads to manual verification processes, increased friction, and limited access to benefits.
Operationally, organisations must manage heterogeneous identity systems and adapt to new trust frameworks introduced by the EUDIW. This includes onboarding wallet-compatible infrastructure, aligning with **eIDAS 2.0 requirements**, and ensuring acceptance of credentials issued by diverse providers.
Pilot implementations will therefore need to operate within these constraints, addressing interoperability gaps, supporting gradual adoption, and ensuring compatibility with both legacy systems and emerging EUDIW standards.


**UC7-UC8:**
------------------------
Considering the actual absence of any identity matching on UC7 and UC8 real processes, PagoPA and Trenitalia need to re-design the scenario including the Wallet interactions in their actual processes.
In order to avoid difficulties or critical issues in the production systems and consequences on Italian real users, UC7 and UC8 will be piloted in  pre-production using fake mock data. PagoPA will develop a Test App (PagoPA EUDIW), in which testers can try the experience of presenting their credentials via that EUDIW to the Trenitalia app, which will verify their status and deliver the service.

**UC9**
------------------
The current solution relying on a 3rd PVID party provider and requires traveler to perform many steps like manually entering data, scanning their id document and performing face biometric and liveness check in order to requests their biometric profile and ready to travel attestation. This system adds friction to the process and some traveller can abandoned it without completion because it is too long or tedious.



**UC1**
------------------


- **Added end‑user friction on smartphone**
  - During boarding with **EUDIW**, the traveler must:
    - Review and accept a **selective disclosure request**.
    - Explicitly provide consent for data sharing.
    - **Authenticate on the phone** (PIN or biometrics) to enable a **1:1 identity match** before sending data.
  - These steps are new compared to traditional boarding flows.

- **Redundancy of identity checks**
  - The **1:1 match performed on the smartphone** is often followed by:
    - A **second 1:1 face match at the boarding gate** (eGate or agent‑assisted).
  - From the traveler’s perspective, this duplication is hard to justify and perceived as redundant.

- **User experience degradation**
  - The accumulation of consent screens, authentication prompts, and repeated face checks:
    - Breaks the “passive” boarding experience users are accustomed to.
    - Contradicts expectations of speed and simplicity.
  - Instead of feeling frictionless, digital identity risks being perceived as **more complex than showing a physical ID**.

- **Adoption risk**
  - Travelers may not understand:
    - Why multiple identity confirmations are needed.
    - Why digital identity feels more demanding than manual checks.
  - Without clear and immediate benefits (faster boarding, fewer stops), this **new friction threatens user adoption**.
