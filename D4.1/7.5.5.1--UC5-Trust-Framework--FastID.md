#### Trust Framework

In UC5 trust is based on whether the stadium access systems can rely on the credentials presented through the FastID wallet to grant access to stadium areas.

The approach follows the eIDAS 2.0 / EUDI Wallet trust principles, where relying parties do not establish individual trust relationships with each issuer, instead they rely on a shared framework that allows them to verify whether a credential can be accepted at the time of use.

In practice the RP validates:
- That the DTC originates from a recognised wallet (Masterlist)
- The access credential is issued within the trusted live setup
- None of the credentials have expired or been revoked
- That the data presented matches the access context

For the pilot this is implemented through a controlled Trust List. It allows the system to simulate issuer recognition and trust decisions in a way that reflects the expected production model, while still being aligned with the existing live FastID environment.

Trust checks are part of the normal access flow and are performed at each verification point, either directly by the stadium access system or through a supporting verification service.

Overall, UC5 relies on a simple trust chain (PID → DTC → Access Credential), which is validated consistently across the journey and aligned with the general direction of the EUDI Wallet trust framework.
