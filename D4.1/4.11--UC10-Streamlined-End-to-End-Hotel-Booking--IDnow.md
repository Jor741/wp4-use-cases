## 4.11 UC 10: Streamlined End-to-End Hotel Booking (IDnow)

### 4.11.1 Summary

UC 10 demonstrates an end-to-end, paperless hotel journey built around the European Digital Identity Wallet (EUDIW). A traveller books a hotel room through an online booking platform, verifies their identity at booking and at check-in using Personal Identification Data (PID) credentials presented from the EUDIW, receives a Booking Confirmation EAA and — at arrival — a short-lived Digital Room Key EAA and a Guest Summary EAA, and finally fulfils the legal guest-registration obligation by sharing the Guest Summary with the competent national authority. All four interaction points (booking-time identity verification, check-in identity verification, door unlock, and authority reporting) are mediated by the wallet under explicit, per-presentation user consent. The use case is deliberately designed to exercise both online (web-based, cross-border) and proximity (QR/NFC/BLE, partially offline) verification flows, and to combine PID with three IDnow-issued EAAs in SD-JWT VC format over OIDC4VCI / OID4VP. UC 10 is led by IDnow GmbH as QTSP and EAA Issuer, in cooperation with a booking-platform partner and a hotel partner inside the APTITUDE consortium.

### 4.11.2 User story

> "As a hotel guest, I want to book a hotel room, verify my identity, receive a digital room key, and share the required guest registration data with the competent authority — all through my EUDI Wallet — so that I can enjoy a seamless, privacy-preserving, paperless stay without queuing at reception, carrying a physical ID, or filling in a paper registration form."

A complementary secondary user story applies to the hotel operator:

> "As a hotelier, I want to onboard guests, hand over room access, and meet my legal guest-registration duties on the basis of cryptographically verifiable wallet credentials, so that I can reduce front-desk effort, eliminate manual data entry errors, and demonstrate compliance with national hotel-registration law."

### 4.11.3 Actors (summary)

| Entity / Role | Description |
| --- | --- |
| User (Guest) | EU resident travelling within the EU; holder of an EUDIW with a valid PID |
| PID Issuer | National PID issuer of the guest's Member State (federated via APTITUDE WP2 trust framework) |
| Booking Platform (RP1) | Online travel/booking platform; verifies PID at booking time and triggers issuance of the Booking Confirmation EAA |
| Hotel (RP2) | Hotel front-desk / check-in system; verifies PID and Booking Confirmation EAA at arrival, triggers issuance of Digital Room Key EAA and Guest Summary EAA |
| Door Lock / Reader (RP3) | Proximity verifier at the room door; verifies the Digital Room Key EAA, potentially offline |
| Competent Authority (RP4) | National authority receiving guest registration data under EU Regulation 2025/12 and national hotel-registration law |
| Credential Issuer (QTSP) | IDnow GmbH — issues the Booking Confirmation, Digital Room Key, and Guest Summary EAAs as SD-JWT VCs under a qualified electronic seal |
| EUDIW Provider | Wallet vendor providing the certified EUDIW instance used by the guest |
| Trust Infrastructure | APTITUDE pilot Trusted Issuer List (TIL) and federated national TILs (see Section 8) |

### 4.11.4 Main flow

The journey is structured in five phases, each anchored on a wallet interaction:

1. **Phase 1 — Booking and PID verification.** The guest selects a room on the Booking Platform and is prompted to authenticate via the EUDIW. The platform issues an OID4VP request for PID attributes (family name, given name, date of birth, nationality). After consent and biometric/PIN authentication, the guest releases the PID Verifiable Presentation; the platform validates issuer signature and key binding against the federated TIL.
2. **Phase 2 — Booking Confirmation EAA issuance.** Upon successful payment, the Booking Platform triggers IDnow (QTSP) to issue a Booking Confirmation EAA via OIDC4VCI pre-authorised code flow. The EAA is bound to the wallet instance and stored in the EUDIW.
3. **Phase 3 — Hotel check-in.** At arrival, the guest scans a QR code at the kiosk/front desk. The hotel RP requests both PID and Booking Confirmation EAA; after consent, the wallet returns a combined Verifiable Presentation. The hotel matches the PID identity to the booking record.
4. **Phase 4 — Digital Room Key and Guest Summary issuance.** The hotel system triggers IDnow to issue, in a single batch transaction, the Digital Room Key EAA (containing the `lock_mac_key` and short-lived `exp` claim) and the Guest Summary EAA (containing the consolidated guest-registration dataset). At the door, the wallet presents the Room Key EAA over NFC/BLE to the reader, which verifies the credential — potentially fully offline — and unlocks the door.
5. **Phase 5 — Authority reporting.** The hotel forwards the Guest Summary EAA (or a derived presentation, depending on national authority interface) to the competent authority to discharge its registration duty. User consent is captured at the moment the Guest Summary is generated in the wallet.

Fallbacks (paper registration form, manual key card issuance, manual identity check) are documented per phase under the Unhappy Paths and are summarised in Section 9.

### 4.11.5 Success criteria and KPIs

- The guest can complete the entire booking → check-in → room access → authority reporting journey end-to-end with the EUDIW as the sole identity and credential carrier.
- All four wallet interactions enforce explicit per-presentation consent with biometric/PIN authentication, and no silent or background data sharing occurs.
- IDnow-issued EAAs (Booking Confirmation, Digital Room Key, Guest Summary) are correctly verified by the Booking Platform, hotel system, door reader, and authority interface, including in cross-border scenarios (guest's PID issued in a different Member State than the hotel).
- The Digital Room Key EAA is verified offline at the door reader using cached issuer keys, within an `exp` window aligned with the booked stay.
- **KPI targets:**
  - ≥ 1 PID issuer integrated.
  - ≥ 3 EAAs issued by IDnow per pilot guest (Booking Confirmation, Digital Room Key, Guest Summary).
  - ≥ 90 % of test sessions completed without falling back to paper/manual processes.
  - Door unlock latency at the reader ≤ 2 seconds in offline mode.
