## Roadmap Towards D4.2 (Implementation Manual)

This section describes the planned activities and the timeline that will carry WP4 from the UC specifications captured in D4.1 to the implementation manual due as **D4.2 — UC Full Pilots and Technical and Implementation Manual** at **Milestone 15 (M18)**. 

### Purpose of the roadmap

D4.1 specifies *what* WP4 will pilot and *why*. D4.2's outcome will specify *how* the pilots will be built. The roadmap presented here identifies the activities of Task T4.3 (Implementation Support), the partners responsible for each activity, the dependencies on other Work Packages, and the intermediate checkpoints.

### Task T4.3 — Implementation Support

Under the Grant Agreement, Task T4.3 is led by GRNET as the WP4 coordinator and runs throughout the 10-month period from the closure of T4.2 to the production of D4.2. Its objective is to provide the implementation framework and the technical-and-functional support that UC leads and their partners need in order to build the pilot. T4.3 has the following main strands of work.

* **Translation of the UC specifications into implementation artefacts.** Each UC lead translates the specification sheet from Annex A into the artefacts needed to build the pilot: API contracts, data-model definitions, sequence diagrams, integration plans, and test scripts. The standardised template along with the Mermaid diagrammatic standards introduced in Section 3.4, will be reused here so that every UC produces artefacts in the same shape.
* **Integration with the WP2 testbed.** Every wallet, credential issuer, and relying party in WP4 is integrated with the WP2 interoperability testbed before any live pilot test is scheduled. T4.3 includes the conformance and interoperability testing campaigns required to obtain the WP2 testbed sign-off for each UC.
* **Coordination with WP3 (DTC) and WP6 (PBEE Framework).** The hand-off points identified in Section 4.1 and Chapter 7 are concretised during T4.3. For each UC that consumes a DTC (summarized in Chapter 4.1), the technical interface with WP3 is finalised and tested. For each UC that includes a payment step (also summarized in Chapter 4.1), the integration with the WP6 PBEE Framework is implemented and verified.
* **Onboarding of Member State wallet providers and PID providers.** The TBC entries in the Section 8.1 mapping are resolved during T4.3 by securing wallet-provider and PID-provider participation in each pilot Member State. This is the critical-path activity of T4.3, because without a live wallet instance in each participating Member State the UC flows cannot be exercised end to end.
* **Closure of the legal-baseline gaps.** The gaps identified in Section 6.2.1 (national hotel guest-registration laws), Section 6.2.2 (rail passenger rights), and Section 6.2.3 (ESCI, ERUA governance, national student-status recognition rules, the Canadian inventory for UC 11) are closed during the early phase of T4.3, ahead of the implementation work that depends on each gap.
* **Preparation of the piloting environment.** T4.3 establishes the environments (pre-production and production-equivalent), the testing strategy and data, the whole testing infrastructure, the user-recruitment procedures, the consent forms, and the support channels that the piloting phase (T4.4) will rely on.
* **Technical and functional support to UC leads.** Throughout T4.3, GRNET provides ongoing support to UC leads on questions of architecture, integration, and compliance, and acts as the liaison point with WP2, WP3, and WP6. Finally compliance questions and concerns will be channeled and discussed with WP7.

### Indicative timeline towards M18

The Grant Agreement places D4.2 at **Milestone 15 (M18)**. The intermediate checkpoints below are indicative and will be refined during the early phase of T4.3.

| Period | Activity |
|---|---|
| Closure of T4.2 → start of T4.3 **M9** | Hand-off of D4.1, freezing of the UC specification baseline, kick-off of T4.3 with all UC leads. |
| Early T4.3 | Closure of the legal-baseline gaps. Resolution of the Section 8.1 TBC entries (wallet provider and PID provider per pilot Member State). |
| Mid T4.3 | UC-by-UC translation of specifications into implementation artefacts. First integration tests on the WP2 testbed. WP3 and WP6 interface specifications finalised. |
| Pre-M18 | UC-level implementation freeze. Final WP2 testbed sign-off per UC. Compilation and internal review of D4.2. |
| **M18** | **Submission of D4.2 (Milestone 15).** |

The activities above run in parallel rather than strictly in sequence. UC-specific schedules are expected to adjust to the maturity of each UC and responding external environment changes (EU regulations, etc.). 

### Hand-offs and responsibilities

The transition from D4.1 to D4.2 involves three categories of actors with distinct responsibilities.

* **UC leads** are responsible for the UC-level implementation chapters of D4.2 and for the corresponding integration work. They translate the specification sheet of their UC into the artefacts listed above, lead the integration with WP2/WP3/WP6, onboard the partners they engage, and produce the per-UC content of D4.2.
* The **WP4 coordinator (GRNET)** is responsible for the cross-cutting implementation chapters, for the consistency of D4.2 across UCs, for the coordination with WP2/WP3/WP6 at the work-package level, for the management of the open items recorded in Section 10.2, and for the assembly and review of D4.2.
* **Other Work Packages** contribute through the technical interfaces that they own:
  * **WP2** provides the testbed and the trust infrastructure on which the WP4 implementations are validated, and confirms the conformance status of each UC implementation before the pilot phase.
  * **WP3** provides the DTC issuance and verification components that the relevant WP4 UCs consume, with the technical interfaces finalised during T4.3.
  * **WP6** provides the PBEE Framework that the WP4 UCs with payment steps invoke, with the integration finalised during T4.3.
  * **WP7** reviews the personal-data aspects of each UC implementation, including the DPIAs and the consent flows that will be used during the piloting phase.

### Pre-conditions for entering D4.2 work

The following pre-conditions must be met before the per-UC implementation work can start in earnest. Each is assigned to a primary owner; the consolidated tracking is part of Section 10.2.

* **Stable UC specification baseline.** The UC specification sheets in Annex A of D4.1 must be frozen at the closure of T4.2. UC leads own this pre-condition.
* **Wallet-provider and PID-provider commitments per pilot Member State.** Each pilot Member State must have at least one identified wallet provider and PID provider committed to participate. UC leads, working with GRNET, own this pre-condition.
* **Closure of the legal-baseline gaps that affect a UC.** The relevant national instruments and sector-specific regulations identified in Section 10.2 must be added to the consolidated regulation list before the UC's implementation chapter is drafted. UC leads own this pre-condition for the regulations in their sector.
* **WP2 testbed availability and profile freeze.** WP4 will base its work on technical profiles published by WP2.
* **WP3 and WP6 interface readiness.** The DTC and PBEE Framework interfaces consumed by the relevant WP4 UCs must reach a state at which they can be integrated against. 

### Risks specific to the D4.1 → D4.2 transition

Two risks specific to the transition deserve a dedicated mention; they are treated alongside the other WP4 risks in Section 9.1.

* **Late onboarding of wallet providers and PID providers.** Without committed Member State actors, the UCs cannot be exercised end to end during the pilot. The mitigation is to start the onboarding work in the very early phase of T4.3, with UC leads supported by GRNET, and to escalate to the European Commission and to Member State authorities if commitments do not materialise on the planned schedule.
* **Drift of the WP2 technical profiles.** A change in the WP2 profiles after UC implementations have started carries a non-trivial cost. The mitigation is a tight liaison between WP4 and WP2 throughout T4.3, with version-aware design of the WP4 implementations so that they can absorb minor profile updates without redesign.

### Outlook beyond D4.2

D4.2 is the second major checkpoint of WP4. The pilots described in D4.2 will be executed during T4.4 and reported on in D4.3 (Validation Report) at **Milestone 16 (M22)**. The roadmap towards D4.3 is the subject of Section 10.4.
