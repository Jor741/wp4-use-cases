#### Electronic Attestations of Attributes (EAA / QEAA)

The main credential used in this use case is the football ticket or access credential, issued as a non-qualified Electronic Attestation of Attributes (EAA).

This credential is issued by the football club through the FastID setup used in the pilot. It represents the access rights of the holder to specific stadium locations and is linked to the verified identity through the DTC.

The DTC (Digital Travel Credential) is used as an interrmediate step in the flow, to allow selective disclosure. It is created from the PID and used as the basis for issuing the football ticket or access credential. This allows the system to rely on a verified identity without repeating the full identity verification at each access point.

The intended verifier is the stadium access system, which requests the credential from the wallet and validates it before granting access. Depending on the access flow, biometric verification can also be used during entry.

The following attributes will define the EAA
- family_name
- access_rights
- validity_period
- portrait (Optional for biometrics)
